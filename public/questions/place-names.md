# Category: Place Names

## E1
Q: Which continent shares its name with a princess in ancient Greek mythology?
A) Asia
B) Africa
C) Europe
D) America
Answer: C
Fact: In Greek mythology, Europa was a Phoenician princess carried off by Zeus in the form of a bull — her name became associated with the western lands and eventually the continent.

## E2
Q: What does 'Buenos Aires' mean in Spanish?
A) Blue Waters
B) Golden Plains
C) Good Winds
D) Fair Skies
Answer: C
Fact: Spanish sailors named the port 'Ciudad de la Santísima Trinidad y Puerto de Santa María de los Buenos Aires', later shortened to Buenos Aires.

## E3
Q: What are people from the Netherlands formally called in English?
A) Hollanders
B) Netherlandish
C) Dutch
D) Flemish
Answer: C
Fact: The word 'Dutch' originally referred to all Germanic-speaking peoples before narrowing to mean specifically those from the Netherlands.

## E4
Q: What was Zimbabwe formerly known as under colonial rule?
A) Rhodesia
B) Zambia
C) Tanganyika
D) Nyasaland
Answer: A
Fact: Rhodesia was named after Cecil Rhodes, the British colonial magnate who orchestrated British expansion in southern Africa.

## E5
Q: Which country's name comes from the Latin word for silver?
A) Chile
B) Brazil
C) Peru
D) Argentina
Answer: D
Fact: Argentina derives from 'argentum', Latin for silver, because early explorers hoped to find silver deposits in the region.

## E6
Q: Istanbul was previously known by which famous name?
A) Ankara
B) Constantinople
C) Antioch
D) Smyrna
Answer: B
Fact: Constantinople was the capital of the Byzantine Empire for over a thousand years before the Ottoman conquest in 1453.

## E7
Q: Which country is named after the Equator that runs through it?
A) Colombia
B) Ecuador
C) Panama
D) Bolivia
Answer: B
Fact: Ecuador is the Spanish word for equator, and the country is one of the few nations named directly after a geographical feature.

## E8
Q: What language does the city name 'Los Angeles' come from?
A) Portuguese
B) French
C) Italian
D) Spanish
Answer: D
Fact: Los Angeles is short for 'El Pueblo de Nuestra Señora la Reina de los Ángeles', meaning 'The Town of Our Lady the Queen of the Angels'.

## E9
Q: What are people from Finland correctly called in English?
A) Fins
B) Finnics
C) Finns
D) Finlanders
Answer: C
Fact: The Finns call their own country 'Suomi' and themselves 'Suomalaiset' — the name Finland was given to them by outside peoples.

## E10
Q: What does 'Rio de Janeiro' mean in Portuguese?
A) City of Gold
B) River of January
C) Great Harbour
D) Southern Bay
Answer: B
Fact: Portuguese explorers arrived at Guanabara Bay in January 1502 and mistook it for the mouth of a great river, naming it Rio de Janeiro — 'River of January'.

## M1
Q: What does 'Kuala Lumpur' mean in Malay?
A) Silver River
B) Muddy Confluence
C) Eastern Port
D) Great Jungle
Answer: B
Fact: Kuala Lumpur was named for the muddy meeting point of the Gombak and Klang rivers where tin miners first settled in the 1850s.

## M2
Q: Sri Lanka was known by which colonial name until 1972?
A) Malacca
B) Saigon
C) Ceylon
D) Rangoon
Answer: C
Fact: Ceylon tea remains the internationally recognised name for Sri Lankan tea, even decades after the country officially changed its name.

## M3
Q: What does 'Reykjavik' mean in Old Norse?
A) Frozen Bay
B) Smoky Cove
C) Cold Harbour
D) North Shore
Answer: B
Fact: Norse settler Ingólfur Arnarson named the spot 'Reykjavík' after seeing steam rising from nearby geothermal hot springs.

## M4
Q: What was Ho Chi Minh City called before 1976?
A) Hanoi
B) Saigon
C) Hue
D) Da Nang
Answer: B
Fact: Saigon was renamed Ho Chi Minh City in 1976 after North and South Vietnam reunified, honouring their revolutionary leader.

## M5
Q: Which country's name derives from an indigenous word meaning 'land of many waters'?
A) Bangladesh
B) Guyana
C) Paraguay
D) Uruguay
Answer: B
Fact: Guyana is derived from a Guiana indigenous term meaning 'land of many waters', reflecting the country's vast river systems and wetlands.

## M6
Q: Myanmar was known internationally by which other name until 1989?
A) Siam
B) Indochina
C) Burma
D) Malaya
Answer: C
Fact: The military junta changed the country's English name from Burma to Myanmar in 1989; many governments and groups still use Burma.

## M7
Q: What are people from Luxembourg correctly called?
A) Luxembourgish
B) Luxembourgers
C) Luxembois
D) Luxians
Answer: B
Fact: Luxembourg is one of the world's only Grand Duchies, and its residents speak three official languages: Luxembourgish, German, and French.

## M8
Q: Which African country is named after its great river, whose name means 'great river' in local Bantu languages?
A) Nigeria
B) Senegal
C) Congo
D) Cameroon
Answer: C
Fact: The Congo River gets its name from the Bakongo kingdom; 'Kongo' is thought to refer to the mountains or the hunting people of the region.

## M9
Q: What does 'Oslo' most likely mean in Old Norse?
A) High Point
B) Cold River
C) God's Meadow
D) River Mouth
Answer: C
Fact: Oslo's name likely comes from Old Norse 'Áss' (god) and 'ló' (meadow), making it roughly 'the meadow of the gods'.

## M10
Q: What informal demonym do New Zealanders use for themselves?
A) Zealanders
B) Kiwis
C) Maoris
D) Antipodeans
Answer: B
Fact: Kiwi became the informal demonym for New Zealanders after the kiwi, a flightless bird native only to New Zealand.

## H1
Q: What was Iran officially called in the West before 1935?
A) Mesopotamia
B) Parthia
C) Persia
D) Assyria
Answer: C
Fact: The Iranian government formally requested the international community use 'Iran' in 1935; 'Persia' had been used in the West since ancient Greek times.

## H2
Q: What does 'Nairobi' mean in the Maasai language?
A) Red Earth
B) Cool Waters
C) High Ground
D) Open Plains
Answer: B
Fact: Nairobi grew from a Maasai watering hole called 'Enkare Nairobi' meaning 'cool waters', used as a British railway depot in 1899.

## H3
Q: The name 'Pakistan' is an acronym — which region does the 'K' represent?
A) Kabul
B) Karachi
C) Kashmir
D) Khyber
Answer: C
Fact: Choudhry Rahmat Ali coined 'Pakistan' in 1933 from Punjab, Afghania, Kashmir, Sindh, and Baluchistan — with 'istan' meaning land.

## H4
Q: What was Malawi called before independence in 1964?
A) Bechuanaland
B) Nyasaland
C) Basutoland
D) Tanganyika
Answer: B
Fact: Nyasaland took its name from Lake Nyasa (now Lake Malawi), with 'nyasa' meaning 'lake' in the local Bantu language.

## H5
Q: The name 'Cameroon' derives from a Portuguese word meaning what?
A) Red River
B) Prawns
C) Forest Land
D) Rocky Shore
Answer: B
Fact: Portuguese explorers named the Wouri River 'Rio dos Camarões' (River of Prawns) in the 1400s, and the name eventually became the country's.

## H6
Q: What was Kolkata officially called under British colonial rule?
A) Bombay
B) Madras
C) Calcutta
D) Poona
Answer: C
Fact: Kolkata was officially renamed from Calcutta in 2001 to better reflect the Bengali pronunciation that locals had used for centuries.

## H7
Q: What does 'Ulaanbaatar', the capital of Mongolia, mean in Mongolian?
A) Eternal City
B) Northern Star
C) Red Hero
D) Golden Steppe
Answer: C
Fact: Ulaanbaatar was renamed from 'Urga' to 'Red Hero' in 1924 following the Mongolian People's Revolution inspired by Soviet Communism.

## H8
Q: What are people from Niger (the country) correctly called?
A) Nigerians
B) Nigerois
C) Nigeriens
D) Nigérans
Answer: C
Fact: Nigeriens (from Niger) are frequently confused with Nigerians (from Nigeria); they are entirely separate countries with different histories.

## H9
Q: The name 'Belize' is thought to derive from a Mayan word meaning what?
A) Muddy Water
B) Sunrise Land
C) Jaguar Forest
D) Sacred River
Answer: A
Fact: One leading theory links 'Belize' to the Mayan word 'Balix' meaning 'muddy water', referencing the Belize River that flows through the country.

## H10
Q: What was the country of Burkina Faso called before 1984?
A) Dahomey
B) Upper Volta
C) Gold Coast
D) Ivory Republic
Answer: B
Fact: Renamed by revolutionary leader Thomas Sankara, 'Burkina Faso' means 'land of incorruptible people' in Mooré and Dioula languages.
