## M1
Q: Which part of the panda is an evolutionary adaptation to help it consume more food?
A) Belly
B) Mouth
C) Tail
D) Thumb
Answer: D
Fact: It's "thumb" is actually just an enlarged wrist bone that allows the panda to hold on to it's favourite food - Bamboo

## M2
Q: Which of the following did not belong to the Sauropod family?
A) Agilisaurus
B) Brontosaurus
C) Brachiosaurus
D) Diplodocus
Answer: A
Fact: Sauropods are large plant-eating dinosaurs with long necks and tails, small heads, and stumpy legs. The Agilisaurus was one of the first Ornithopods, which were bipedal and whose name means "Agile Lizard".

## M3
Q: On which continent have dinosaur fossils never been found?
A) Antarctica
B) Asia
C) Australia
D) None of them
Answer: D
Fact: It has been theorized that during the Triassic period, all the continents were arranged as a single continent called Pangea. As they drifted apart, dinosaurs evolved and spread across the globe even as far as Antarctica.

## H1
Q: Which one of these prehistoirc geologic time periods is the oldest?
A) Cenozoic
B) Mesozoic
C) Paleozoic
D) Proterozoic
Answer: D
Fact: Translated in Greek to mean "before life", the Proterozoic period is dated around 2500 to 500m years ago, with the other three falling into the Phanerozoic eon and ending with the emergence of human life.

## H2
Q: Which of the following are not on the same evolutionary tree branch?
A) Bears
B) Hyenas
C) Seals
D) Wolves
Answer: B
Fact: Caniformia is the carnivorous suborder that consists of dog-like carnivorans, including dogs, bears, raccoons and mustelids (like badgers, otters and wolverines). Hyenas are Feliformia, which are the cat-like carnivorans, including mongooses and viverrids like civet cats.

## H3
Q: In 1861, the fossil of an Archaeopteryx was found. What was this most like?
A) Bird
B) Fish
C) Insect
D) Mammal
Answer: A
Fact: With feathers and a long, bony tail, Archaeopteryx translates to "First Bird", and is said to be the first evidence supporting the theory of evolution (by linking birds and reptiles)
