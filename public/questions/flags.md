# Category: Flags

## E1
Q: Which country's flag has a red circle on a white background?
A) South Korea
B) Japan
C) Bangladesh
D) Canada
Answer: B
Fact: Japan's flag is called the Nisshōki (sun flag) and the red disc represents the rising sun.

## E2
Q: Which country's flag features a maple leaf?
A) Australia
B) New Zealand
C) Canada
D) Ireland
Answer: C
Fact: Canada's maple leaf flag was adopted in 1965, replacing a design that featured the British Union Jack.

## E3
Q: What are the three colours on the French flag, from left to right?
A) Red, White, Blue
B) Blue, White, Red
C) Blue, Red, White
D) White, Blue, Red
Answer: B
Fact: France's tricolour was adopted during the French Revolution and became a global symbol of republicanism.

## E4
Q: Which country's flag features a crescent moon and a single star on a red background?
A) Saudi Arabia
B) Turkey
C) Iran
D) Egypt
Answer: B
Fact: The crescent and star on Turkey's flag are ancient symbols that predate Islam in that region.

## E5
Q: Which country's flag is the only national flag that is not rectangular?
A) Switzerland
B) Nepal
C) Vatican City
D) Qatar
Answer: B
Fact: Nepal's double-pennant flag is the only non-rectangular national flag in the world.

## E6
Q: Which country's flag has a Union Jack in its top-left corner AND four red stars?
A) Australia
B) New Zealand
C) Fiji
D) Tuvalu
Answer: B
Fact: New Zealand's four red stars represent the Southern Cross constellation visible from the Southern Hemisphere.

## E7
Q: What two colours make up the flag of Vatican City?
A) Red and Gold
B) White and Blue
C) Yellow and White
D) White and Green
Answer: C
Fact: The Vatican's yellow and white flag colours represent the gold and silver keys of Saint Peter.

## E8
Q: Which country's flag is a plain white cross on a square red background?
A) Denmark
B) Norway
C) Switzerland
D) England
Answer: C
Fact: Switzerland's flag is one of only two square national flags in the world, the other being Vatican City.

## E9
Q: What colour is the background of the German flag's top stripe?
A) Red
B) Gold
C) White
D) Black
Answer: D
Fact: Germany's flag has three horizontal stripes: black on top, red in the middle, and gold on the bottom.

## E10
Q: What colour is the background of Bangladesh's flag?
A) White
B) Red
C) Green
D) Blue
Answer: C
Fact: Bangladesh's green background represents the lush landscape of the country, and the red disc symbolises the rising sun.

## M1
Q: How many stars appear on the flag of the United States?
A) 48
B) 52
C) 50
D) 51
Answer: C
Fact: Each star represents a US state; the current 50-star flag has been in use since Hawaii joined in 1960.

## M2
Q: Which country's flag features an eagle holding a snake in its beak?
A) Colombia
B) Mexico
C) Ecuador
D) Peru
Answer: B
Fact: Mexico's eagle and snake emblem is based on an Aztec legend about where to found their capital city.

## M3
Q: Which country's flag features a cedar tree in the centre?
A) Jordan
B) Syria
C) Lebanon
D) Iraq
Answer: C
Fact: The Cedar of Lebanon is one of the world's oldest national symbols, representing peace and immortality.

## M4
Q: How many stars are on the flag of China?
A) 4
B) 6
C) 3
D) 5
Answer: D
Fact: China's five stars represent the Communist Party and the four social classes it united after the 1949 revolution.

## M5
Q: Which country's flag has a dragon on it?
A) Nepal
B) Bhutan
C) Sri Lanka
D) Myanmar
Answer: B
Fact: Bhutan's flag dragon is called Druk (thunder dragon), which is also the Bhutanese name for their country.

## M6
Q: Which country's flag shows a sun with a human face?
A) Uruguay
B) Bolivia
C) Argentina
D) Ecuador
Answer: C
Fact: Argentina's 'Sun of May' celebrates the May Revolution of 1810 and also appears on Uruguay's flag.

## M7
Q: How many horizontal stripes are on the flag of the United States?
A) 15
B) 12
C) 13
D) 14
Answer: C
Fact: The 13 stripes represent the original 13 colonies that declared independence from Britain in 1776.

## M8
Q: What colour is the star on the flag of Somalia?
A) Yellow
B) Green
C) Red
D) White
Answer: D
Fact: Somalia's five-pointed white star on a light blue background represents the five regions historically inhabited by Somali people.

## M9
Q: Which country's flag has horizontal stripes of red, white, and blue — in that order from top to bottom?
A) France
B) Netherlands
C) Russia
D) Luxembourg
Answer: B
Fact: The Dutch tricolour (red, white, blue from top) is one of the oldest tricolour flags and inspired many others worldwide.

## M10
Q: Which country's flag is green and yellow and features the Southern Cross constellation?
A) South Africa
B) Jamaica
C) Brazil
D) Australia
Answer: C
Fact: Brazil's flag shows the night sky over Rio de Janeiro on 15 November 1889, the day the republic was proclaimed.

## H1
Q: Which country's flag features an AK-47 rifle?
A) Angola
B) Zimbabwe
C) Ethiopia
D) Mozambique
Answer: D
Fact: Mozambique's flag is the only national flag in the world to depict a modern automatic firearm.

## H2
Q: How many stars appear on the flag of Australia in total?
A) 5
B) 7
C) 6
D) 8
Answer: C
Fact: Australia's flag has five Southern Cross stars plus one large Commonwealth Star below the Union Jack.

## H3
Q: What are the two colours that diagonally divide Bhutan's flag?
A) Red and Blue
B) Green and White
C) Orange and Yellow
D) Red and White
Answer: C
Fact: Bhutan's orange half represents Buddhism and the yellow half represents civil authority and the royal family.

## H4
Q: Which country's flag is nearly identical to Monaco's, differing only in proportions?
A) Malta
B) Poland
C) Georgia
D) Indonesia
Answer: D
Fact: Both Indonesia and Monaco have a red-over-white bicolour, but their flag dimensions differ slightly.

## H5
Q: Which country's flag features a broken trident on it?
A) Sri Lanka
B) Trinidad
C) Barbados
D) Mauritius
Answer: C
Fact: The Barbados trident's middle prong is deliberately broken to symbolise the country's break from colonial rule.

## H6
Q: What colour is the BOTTOM stripe on the flag of Lithuania?
A) Green
B) White
C) Blue
D) Red
Answer: D
Fact: Lithuania's three horizontal stripes are yellow, green, and red — representing the sun, forests, and the blood of patriots.

## H7
Q: Which country's flag features a cog wheel and a machete?
A) Mozambique
B) Angola
C) Zimbabwe
D) Zambia
Answer: B
Fact: Angola's flag has a black cog wheel (representing industry) and a red machete (representing agriculture and the armed struggle) in its centre.

## H8
Q: Which country's flag has a black five-pointed star on a yellow stripe between two green stripes?
A) Equatorial Guinea
B) Guinea-Bissau
C) Congo
D) São Tomé and Príncipe
Answer: D
Fact: São Tomé and Príncipe's flag uses Pan-African colours, and its black star symbolises African freedom and unity.

## H9
Q: How many points does each star have on the flag of Morocco?
A) 8
B) 4
C) 6
D) 5
Answer: D
Fact: Morocco's green interlaced pentagram, known as Solomon's Seal, on a red background has been used since the 17th century.

## H10
Q: Which country's flag is a horizontal tricolour of blue, black, and white?
A) Botswana
B) Estonia
C) Sierra Leone
D) Namibia
Answer: A
Fact: Botswana's light blue, black, and white flag represents water (rare and precious in the Kalahari), and the harmony between black and white races.
