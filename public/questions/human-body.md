# Category: Human Body

## E1
Q: Which organ pumps blood around the body?
A) Liver
B) Kidney
C) Heart
D) Lung
Answer: C
Fact: The human heart beats roughly 100,000 times every single day.

## E2
Q: What is the longest bone in the human body?
A) Tibia
B) Humerus
C) Spine
D) Femur
Answer: D
Fact: The femur (thigh bone) makes up roughly a quarter of a person's total height.

## E3
Q: Which organ filters waste from your blood to make urine?
A) Spleen
B) Kidney
C) Liver
D) Bladder
Answer: B
Fact: Each kidney contains about one million tiny filtering units called nephrons.

## E4
Q: What is the largest muscle in the human body?
A) Bicep
B) Quadricep
C) Gluteus Maximus
D) Hamstring
Answer: C
Fact: The gluteus maximus is so large because it keeps your body upright when walking uphill.

## E5
Q: Which organ is responsible for your sense of sight?
A) Nose
B) Ear
C) Skin
D) Eye
Answer: D
Fact: The human eye can distinguish roughly 10 million different colours.

## E6
Q: Which body system includes the brain and spinal cord?
A) Digestive
B) Nervous
C) Circulatory
D) Respiratory
Answer: B
Fact: Nerve signals can travel through the body at speeds of up to 430 kilometres per hour.

## E7
Q: Which organ produces bile to help digest fats?
A) Stomach
B) Pancreas
C) Kidney
D) Liver
Answer: D
Fact: The liver is the only internal organ that can fully regenerate itself after partial removal.

## E8
Q: How many bones does an adult human body have?
A) 156
B) 206
C) 256
D) 300
Answer: B
Fact: Babies are born with around 270 bones; many fuse together as they grow.

## E9
Q: Which organ do you use to breathe?
A) Liver
B) Heart
C) Stomach
D) Lungs
Answer: D
Fact: If you spread out both lungs flat, they would cover roughly the size of a tennis court.

## E10
Q: What is the largest organ of the human body?
A) Liver
B) Skin
C) Intestine
D) Lung
Answer: B
Fact: The average adult's skin weighs around 4 to 5 kilograms and covers about 2 square metres.

## M1
Q: How many pairs of ribs does a human have?
A) 10
B) 11
C) 12
D) 14
Answer: C
Fact: The bottom two pairs are called floating ribs because they only attach to the spine, not the sternum.

## M2
Q: Which blood type is known as the universal donor?
A) AB+
B) O-
C) A+
D) B-
Answer: B
Fact: Only about 7% of the population has O- blood, making donors especially valuable.

## M3
Q: Which organ produces insulin?
A) Liver
B) Spleen
C) Pancreas
D) Adrenal Gland
Answer: C
Fact: Insulin was first successfully used to treat a human patient in January 1922.

## M4
Q: What is the smallest bone in the human body?
A) Malleus
B) Incus
C) Stapes
D) Phalanx
Answer: C
Fact: The stapes (stirrup bone) in the ear is only about 3 millimetres long, smaller than a grain of rice.

## M5
Q: Which body system includes veins, arteries and capillaries?
A) Lymphatic
B) Nervous
C) Circulatory
D) Endocrine
Answer: C
Fact: Laid end to end, the blood vessels in a single human body would stretch about 100,000 kilometres.

## M6
Q: What is the strongest muscle relative to its size in the human body?
A) Tongue
B) Masseter
C) Bicep
D) Calf
Answer: B
Fact: The masseter jaw muscle can close your teeth with a force of up to 90 kilograms.

## M7
Q: Which chamber of the heart pumps blood to the lungs?
A) Left Atrium
B) Left Ventricle
C) Right Atrium
D) Right Ventricle
Answer: D
Fact: The left ventricle has much thicker walls than the right because it must pump blood all the way around the body.

## M8
Q: What is the name of the bone that forms the lower jaw?
A) Maxilla
B) Mandible
C) Zygomatic
D) Temporal
Answer: B
Fact: The mandible is the only movable bone in the skull.

## M9
Q: Which part of the digestive system absorbs most nutrients?
A) Stomach
B) Large Intestine
C) Small Intestine
D) Oesophagus
Answer: C
Fact: The small intestine is about 6 to 7 metres long, far longer than the large intestine despite its name.

## M10
Q: Which cells in the blood carry oxygen around the body?
A) Platelets
B) White Blood Cells
C) Plasma
D) Red Blood Cells
Answer: D
Fact: A single red blood cell completes a full circuit of the body in about 20 seconds.

## H1
Q: What is the medical term for the kneecap?
A) Calcaneus
B) Patella
C) Talus
D) Fibula
Answer: B
Fact: The patella is a sesamoid bone, embedded within a tendon rather than connected by ligaments like most bones.

## H2
Q: Which part of the brain controls balance and coordination?
A) Cerebrum
B) Hypothalamus
C) Medulla
D) Cerebellum
Answer: D
Fact: Though the cerebellum is only 10% of brain volume, it contains about 50% of all the brain's neurons.

## H3
Q: Which gland is known as the master gland of the endocrine system?
A) Thyroid
B) Adrenal
C) Pituitary
D) Pineal
Answer: C
Fact: Despite controlling so many body functions, the pituitary gland is only about the size of a pea.

## H4
Q: How many carpal (wrist) bones does a human hand have?
A) 6
B) 8
C) 10
D) 12
Answer: B
Fact: The eight carpal bones are arranged in two rows and allow the wide range of wrist movements we use daily.

## H5
Q: What is the name of the valve between the left atrium and left ventricle?
A) Tricuspid
B) Aortic
C) Pulmonary
D) Mitral
Answer: D
Fact: The mitral valve is also called the bicuspid valve because it has exactly two flaps (cusps).

## H6
Q: Approximately what percentage of blood volume is made up of plasma?
A) 35%
B) 45%
C) 55%
D) 65%
Answer: C
Fact: Plasma is mostly water but also carries proteins, hormones, and nutrients to cells throughout the body.

## H7
Q: Which bone forms the heel of the foot?
A) Talus
B) Navicular
C) Cuboid
D) Calcaneus
Answer: D
Fact: The calcaneus is the largest bone in the foot and bears most of the body's weight when standing.

## H8
Q: What is the name of the tube connecting each kidney to the bladder?
A) Urethra
B) Ureter
C) Renal Pelvis
D) Vas Deferens
Answer: B
Fact: Peristaltic waves in the ureters push urine into the bladder even if you are standing on your head.

## H9
Q: What is the name of the dome-shaped muscle that separates the chest from the abdomen?
A) Diaphragm
B) Intercostal
C) Trapezius
D) Peritoneum
Answer: A
Fact: The diaphragm contracts with every single breath you take, roughly 20,000 times per day.

## H10
Q: Which protein gives red blood cells their oxygen-carrying ability?
A) Myoglobin
B) Albumin
C) Fibrinogen
D) Haemoglobin
Answer: D
Fact: Each haemoglobin molecule can carry four oxygen molecules, and a single red blood cell contains about 270 million haemoglobin molecules.
