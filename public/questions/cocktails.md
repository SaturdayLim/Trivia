# Category: Cocktails

## E1
Q: Which spirit is the base of a Margarita?
A) Vodka
B) Rum
C) Tequila
D) Gin
Answer: C
Fact: The Margarita is the most popular cocktail in the USA by sales volume.

## E2
Q: A Champagne flute is named after which musical instrument?
A) Trumpet
B) Flute
C) Clarinet
D) Oboe
Answer: B
Fact: The tall narrow shape of a flute glass helps Champagne retain its bubbles for longer.

## E3
Q: What is the main spirit in a Mojito?
A) Tequila
B) Vodka
C) Whiskey
D) Rum
Answer: D
Fact: The Mojito originated in Cuba and was reportedly a favourite drink of Ernest Hemingway.

## E4
Q: Which country is Tequila from?
A) Brazil
B) Mexico
C) Colombia
D) Spain
Answer: B
Fact: By law, Tequila can only be produced in specific regions of Mexico, primarily the state of Jalisco.

## E5
Q: What fruit gives a Pina Colada its name?
A) Lime
B) Mango
C) Pineapple
D) Orange
Answer: C
Fact: Pina Colada means 'strained pineapple' in Spanish and is the national drink of Puerto Rico.

## E6
Q: What grain must Bourbon whiskey be primarily made from?
A) Wheat
B) Barley
C) Rye
D) Corn
Answer: D
Fact: By US law, Bourbon must be made from a mash of at least 51% corn.

## E7
Q: What colour is Grenadine syrup?
A) Yellow
B) Red
C) Blue
D) Green
Answer: B
Fact: Grenadine is named after the French word for pomegranate and was originally made from pomegranate juice.

## E8
Q: Champagne comes from which country?
A) Italy
B) Spain
C) France
D) Germany
Answer: C
Fact: Only sparkling wine from the Champagne region of France can legally be called Champagne.

## E9
Q: Which cocktail is made with vodka, tomato juice, and spices?
A) Screwdriver
B) Harvey Wallbanger
C) Bloody Mary
D) Cosmopolitan
Answer: C
Fact: The Bloody Mary is one of the most customisable cocktails, with hundreds of documented variations worldwide.

## E10
Q: What plant is Tequila distilled from?
A) Cactus
B) Agave
C) Yucca
D) Aloe
Answer: B
Fact: It takes a blue agave plant 8 to 12 years to mature before it can be harvested for Tequila production.

## M1
Q: Which ingredient gives a Cosmopolitan its pink colour?
A) Cointreau
B) Campari
C) Cranberry
D) Aperol
Answer: C
Fact: The Cosmopolitan was popularised in the 1990s partly thanks to its frequent appearances on the TV show Sex and the City.

## M2
Q: The Caipirinha is the national cocktail of which country?
A) Argentina
B) Portugal
C) Brazil
D) Uruguay
Answer: C
Fact: The Caipirinha is made with Cachaca, a spirit distilled from fresh sugarcane juice unique to Brazil.

## M3
Q: What is the key botanical flavour in gin?
A) Coriander
B) Juniper
C) Cardamom
D) Anise
Answer: B
Fact: Gin's name comes from the Dutch word 'jenever', meaning juniper berry.

## M4
Q: Which bitters are used in a classic Old Fashioned?
A) Peychaud's
B) Fernet
C) Campari
D) Angostura
Answer: D
Fact: Angostura bitters were originally created in 1824 as a medicinal tonic in Venezuela, not Trinidad where they are now made.

## M5
Q: A Gin & Tonic is traditionally served in which type of glass?
A) Martini
B) Coupe
C) Highball
D) Snifter
Answer: C
Fact: Highball glasses are named after a railway signal — when the ball was high it meant full steam ahead, time for a quick drink.

## M6
Q: What is the base spirit in a Negroni?
A) Vodka
B) Rum
C) Gin
D) Bourbon
Answer: C
Fact: The Negroni was reputedly invented in Florence in 1919 when Count Camillo Negroni asked for his Americano strengthened with gin.

## M7
Q: Scotch whisky must be aged for at least how many years?
A) 5
B) 3
C) 7
D) 10
Answer: B
Fact: Scotland's Scotch Whisky Regulations require a minimum of three years ageing in oak casks before bottling.

## M8
Q: What herb is essential in a classic Mint Julep?
A) Basil
B) Rosemary
C) Thyme
D) Mint
Answer: D
Fact: The Mint Julep is the official drink of the Kentucky Derby, with around 120,000 served over the two-day race weekend.

## M9
Q: A Sidecar is traditionally served in which glass?
A) Highball
B) Coupe
C) Rocks
D) Collins
Answer: B
Fact: The coupe glass, once used for Champagne, is now the preferred vessel for many classic cocktails including the Daiquiri and Sidecar.

## M10
Q: Cognac brandy is produced in which French region?
A) Burgundy
B) Bordeaux
C) Charente
D) Alsace
Answer: C
Fact: Cognac must be double-distilled in copper pot stills and aged in French oak barrels to bear its protected name.

## H1
Q: Which liqueur is NOT used in a classic Margarita recipe?
A) Triple Sec
B) Cointreau
C) Grand Marnier
D) Amaretto
Answer: D
Fact: Amaretto is an almond-flavoured Italian liqueur with no orange notes, making it incompatible with classic Margarita recipes.

## H2
Q: The Singapore Sling was created at which hotel bar?
A) Mandarin Oriental
B) Raffles
C) Marina Bay Sands
D) Fullerton
Answer: B
Fact: Ngiam Tong Boon created the Singapore Sling around 1915 at the Long Bar inside Raffles Hotel, Singapore.

## H3
Q: Unlike Tequila, Mezcal can be made from how many agave varieties?
A) 1
B) 3
C) 30+
D) 10
Answer: C
Fact: Tequila can only use blue agave, while Mezcal can legally be produced from over 30 different agave species.

## H4
Q: The Daiquiri is named after a Cuban village known for what industry?
A) Fishing
B) Sugar
C) Iron mining
D) Tobacco
Answer: C
Fact: Daiquiri is a small iron-mining village near Santiago de Cuba where American engineer Jennings Cox first mixed the drink around 1898.

## H5
Q: Which French vermouth is most classically associated with a Dry Martini?
A) Martini
B) Noilly Prat
C) Lillet
D) Cinzano
Answer: B
Fact: Noilly Prat is the oldest French dry vermouth, produced since 1813, and is the bartender's preferred choice for a classic Dry Martini.

## H6
Q: The Sazerac cocktail originates from which US city?
A) Chicago
B) Nashville
C) New Orleans
D) Charleston
Answer: C
Fact: The Sazerac was declared the official cocktail of New Orleans by Louisiana state law in 2008 and is considered America's oldest cocktail.

## H7
Q: A Snifter glass is designed to concentrate the aroma of which spirit?
A) Vodka
B) Gin
C) Brandy
D) Rum
Answer: C
Fact: The wide bowl of a snifter allows brandy to be warmed by the hand, releasing volatile aromatics up into its narrow opening.

## H8
Q: Peychaud's Bitters creator Antoine Peychaud originally came from which country?
A) Haiti
B) Jamaica
C) Martinique
D) Guadeloupe
Answer: A
Fact: Antoine Peychaud fled the Haitian Revolution and brought his family bitters recipe to New Orleans in the early 19th century.

## H9
Q: Armagnac is traditionally distilled how many times, unlike Cognac's double distillation?
A) Once
B) Three times
C) Twice
D) Four times
Answer: A
Fact: Armagnac's single distillation produces a more robust and rustic character compared to the smoother double-distilled Cognac.

## H10
Q: The Bobby Burns cocktail uses Scotch, sweet vermouth, and which Scottish liqueur?
A) Glayva
B) Heather Cream
C) Drambuie
D) Athole Brose
Answer: C
Fact: The Bobby Burns is named after Scottish poet Robert Burns and is a stirred cocktail that showcases Scotch with herbal sweetness from Drambuie.
