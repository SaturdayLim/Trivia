# Category: Science

## E1
Q: What is the chemical symbol for gold?
A) Go
B) Gd
C) Au
D) Ag
Answer: C
Fact: Gold's symbol comes from the Latin word 'aurum', meaning shining dawn.

## E2
Q: What gas do plants release during photosynthesis?
A) Carbon dioxide
B) Nitrogen
C) Oxygen
D) Hydrogen
Answer: C
Fact: Nearly all the oxygen in Earth's atmosphere has been produced by photosynthesis over billions of years.

## E3
Q: What force keeps planets in orbit around the Sun?
A) Magnetism
B) Friction
C) Gravity
D) Inertia
Answer: C
Fact: Gravity is the weakest of the four fundamental forces but acts over infinite distances.

## E4
Q: At what temperature does water boil at sea level?
A) 90°C
B) 95°C
C) 100°C
D) 110°C
Answer: C
Fact: Water boils at a lower temperature at high altitudes because atmospheric pressure is reduced.

## E5
Q: Which element is most abundant in Earth's atmosphere?
A) Oxygen
B) Carbon
C) Hydrogen
D) Nitrogen
Answer: D
Fact: Nitrogen makes up about 78% of the air we breathe, while oxygen accounts for only about 21%.

## E6
Q: What is the powerhouse of the cell?
A) Nucleus
B) Mitochondria
C) Ribosome
D) Vacuole
Answer: B
Fact: Mitochondria have their own DNA, evidence they were once independent bacteria absorbed by larger cells.

## E7
Q: What is the chemical formula for water?
A) HO
B) H2O
C) H3O
D) OH2
Answer: B
Fact: Each water molecule is bent at about 104.5 degrees due to lone electron pairs on the oxygen atom.

## E8
Q: Roughly how fast does light travel in a vacuum?
A) 100,000 km/s
B) 200,000 km/s
C) 300,000 km/s
D) 400,000 km/s
Answer: C
Fact: Nothing with mass can reach the speed of light — doing so would require infinite energy.

## E9
Q: What force pulls objects toward the ground on Earth?
A) Magnetism
B) Friction
C) Gravity
D) Inertia
Answer: C
Fact: Without gravity, our atmosphere would drift away into space and oceans would float off the surface.

## E10
Q: What particle has a negative charge in an atom?
A) Proton
B) Neutron
C) Electron
D) Positron
Answer: C
Fact: Electrons are about 1,836 times lighter than protons but carry an equal and opposite charge.

## M1
Q: What is the chemical symbol for iron?
A) Ir
B) In
C) Fe
D) Fo
Answer: C
Fact: Iron's symbol comes from 'ferrum', the Latin name for the metal.

## M2
Q: What is the SI unit of electric current?
A) Volt
B) Watt
C) Ohm
D) Ampere
Answer: D
Fact: The ampere is named after French physicist André-Marie Ampère, a founder of electromagnetism.

## M3
Q: What gas do plants absorb during photosynthesis?
A) Oxygen
B) Nitrogen
C) Hydrogen
D) Carbon dioxide
Answer: D
Fact: Plants convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen using sunlight as energy.

## M4
Q: What is the atomic number of carbon?
A) 4
B) 6
C) 8
D) 12
Answer: B
Fact: Carbon's ability to form four bonds makes it the backbone of all known life on Earth.

## M5
Q: What unit measures the frequency of a wave?
A) Pascal
B) Tesla
C) Hertz
D) Newton
Answer: C
Fact: One hertz means one complete wave cycle per second, named after physicist Heinrich Hertz.

## M6
Q: What is the pH value of a neutral solution?
A) 0
B) 5
C) 7
D) 14
Answer: C
Fact: Pure water has a pH of 7 — below 7 is acidic and above 7 is alkaline (basic).

## M7
Q: Who developed the theory of general relativity?
A) Newton
B) Bohr
C) Einstein
D) Curie
Answer: C
Fact: Einstein published his general theory of relativity in 1915, revolutionising our understanding of gravity.

## M8
Q: What is the chemical symbol for silver?
A) Si
B) Sv
C) Sl
D) Ag
Answer: D
Fact: Silver's symbol Ag comes from 'argentum', the Latin word for silver, which also gives Argentina its name.

## M9
Q: What phenomenon splits white light into a spectrum?
A) Reflection
B) Refraction
C) Diffraction
D) Absorption
Answer: B
Fact: Rainbows form when sunlight refracts into water droplets, reflects internally, then refracts again on exit.

## M10
Q: Which element has the highest melting point?
A) Platinum
B) Iron
C) Tungsten
D) Titanium
Answer: C
Fact: Tungsten melts at 3,422°C, making it the ideal material for light bulb filaments.

## H1
Q: What is the chemical symbol for sodium?
A) So
B) Sd
C) Sm
D) Na
Answer: D
Fact: Sodium's symbol comes from 'natrium', its Latin name derived from the Egyptian word for natural salt.

## H2
Q: What is the approximate half-life of Carbon-14?
A) 1,000 years
B) 5,730 years
C) 10,000 years
D) 50,000 years
Answer: B
Fact: Carbon-14 dating is accurate up to about 50,000 years, beyond which too little C-14 remains to measure.

## H3
Q: How many particles are in one mole of a substance?
A) 6.02 × 10²³
B) 3.01 × 10²³
C) 6.02 × 10²²
D) 9.03 × 10²³
Answer: A
Fact: Avogadro's number was named after Amedeo Avogadro, though he never calculated it himself.

## H4
Q: Who discovered penicillin?
A) Lister
B) Pasteur
C) Fleming
D) Jenner
Answer: C
Fact: Alexander Fleming noticed in 1928 that a Penicillium mould had killed bacteria on a contaminated petri dish.

## H5
Q: Which noble gas is used in neon signs?
A) Argon
B) Krypton
C) Xenon
D) Neon
Answer: D
Fact: Neon produces a distinctive red-orange glow when electrically excited; other gases glow different colours.

## H6
Q: What is the unit of Planck's constant?
A) Joule·second
B) Watt·second
C) Newton·metre
D) Volt·second
Answer: A
Fact: Planck's constant (h ≈ 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s) defines the fundamental scale of quantum effects.

## H7
Q: Which element has atomic number 79?
A) Silver
B) Platinum
C) Gold
D) Mercury
Answer: C
Fact: Gold's atomic number 79 means each atom has 79 protons; its symbol Au comes from Latin 'aurum'.

## H8
Q: What was the first artificial satellite launched into orbit?
A) Explorer 1
B) Vostok
C) Sputnik
D) Cosmos
Answer: C
Fact: Sputnik 1, launched by the Soviet Union in October 1957, began the space age and the space race.

## H9
Q: What process converts glucose into ethanol and CO₂?
A) Oxidation
B) Fermentation
C) Hydrolysis
D) Distillation
Answer: B
Fact: Yeast performs fermentation anaerobically — without oxygen — producing ethanol as a by-product of energy release.

## H10
Q: What is the charge of a neutron?
A) +1
B) -1
C) Neutral
D) +0.5
Answer: C
Fact: Neutrons have no electric charge but are slightly heavier than protons, and free neutrons decay in about 15 minutes.
