# Category: Space

## E1
Q: Which planet is closest to the Sun?
A) Venus
B) Mars
C) Mercury
D) Earth
Answer: C
Fact: Mercury is so close to the Sun that a year there lasts only 88 Earth days.

## E2
Q: Which planet has a famous Great Red Spot?
A) Saturn
B) Jupiter
C) Neptune
D) Uranus
Answer: B
Fact: The Great Red Spot is a storm that has been raging on Jupiter for at least 350 years.

## E3
Q: Which country landed humans on the Moon first?
A) Russia
B) China
C) USA
D) UK
Answer: C
Fact: Apollo 11 landed on the Moon on 20 July 1969, making the USA the first country to do so.

## E4
Q: What is the name of our Sun's galaxy?
A) Andromeda
B) Triangulum
C) Whirlpool
D) Milky Way
Answer: D
Fact: The Milky Way contains an estimated 100 to 400 billion stars.

## E5
Q: Which planet is famous for its beautiful rings?
A) Jupiter
B) Neptune
C) Saturn
D) Uranus
Answer: C
Fact: Saturn's rings are made mostly of ice and rock, and they span up to 282,000 km wide.

## E6
Q: What was the first artificial satellite launched into space?
A) Vostok
B) Explorer
C) Sputnik
D) Luna
Answer: C
Fact: Sputnik 1 was launched by the Soviet Union on 4 October 1957, starting the Space Age.

## E7

Answer: B
Fact: Pluto was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006, leaving our Solar System with eight planets.

## E8

Fact: Neil Armstrong stepped onto the lunar surface on 20 July 1969 during the Apollo 11 mission.

## E9
Q: What type of star is our Sun classified as?
A) Red giant
B) White dwarf
C) Neutron star
D) Yellow dwarf
Answer: D
Fact: Our Sun is classified as a G-type main-sequence star, commonly called a yellow dwarf.

## E10

A) Saturn
B) Uranus

D) Jupiter
Answer: D
Fact: Jupiter is so massive that more than 1,300 Earths could fit inside it.

Fact: Io is the most volcanically active body in the Solar System, with hundreds of active volcanoes.

Q: Which planet rotates on its side with an axial tilt of about 98 degrees?
A) Neptune
B) Saturn
C) Uranus
D) Venus
Answer: C
Fact: Uranus's extreme tilt means its poles experience 42 years of continuous sunlight followed by 42 years of darkness.

Answer: B
Fact: Yuri Gagarin orbited Earth once on 12 April 1961, a journey that lasted 108 minutes.

Fact: Proxima Centauri is about 4.24 light-years away, making it our nearest stellar neighbour.

## H4

A) Rhea
B) Titan
C) Enceladus
D) Mimas
Answer: B
Fact: Titan's lakes are filled with liquid methane and ethane, making it the only body besides Earth with stable surface liquids.

A) Voyager 2
B) Dawn
C) New Horizons

Answer: C
Fact: New Horizons revealed that Pluto has a heart-shaped nitrogen ice plain named Tombaugh Regio.

## H6
Q: The Chandrasekhar limit — maximum mass of a stable white dwarf — is approximately how many solar masses?
A) 1.0
B) 1.4
C) 2.0
D) 3.2
Answer: B
Fact: Above 1.4 solar masses, electron degeneracy pressure can no longer support a white dwarf, leading to a supernova.

## H7

A) Elliptical
B) Barred spiral
C) Irregular
D) Lenticular
Answer: B
Fact: The Milky Way has a central bar-shaped structure of stars from which two main spiral arms extend outward.

Fact: Triton's retrograde orbit suggests it was a captured Kuiper Belt object rather than a moon that formed in place.
