# Category: Inventions

## E1
Q: Who invented the telephone?
A) Edison
B) Bell
C) Morse
D) Tesla
Answer: B
Fact: Alexander Graham Bell made the first telephone call to his assistant Watson on 10 March 1876.

## E2
Q: Which brothers made the first powered aeroplane flight?
A) Lumière
B) Warner
C) Wright
D) Baldwin
Answer: C
Fact: The Wright brothers' first flight at Kitty Hawk in 1903 lasted just 12 seconds.

## E3
Q: Who is credited with inventing the practical incandescent light bulb?
A) Tesla
B) Faraday
C) Edison
D) Swan
Answer: C
Fact: Edison's Menlo Park lab tested over 6,000 materials before settling on carbonised bamboo as the filament.

## E4
Q: Who invented the movable-type printing press in Europe?
A) Gutenberg
B) Caxton
C) Watt
D) Volta
Answer: A
Fact: Gutenberg's press could produce 3,600 pages per workday, replacing months of hand-copying.

## E5
Q: In which country was the World Wide Web invented?
A) USA
B) Germany
C) Japan
D) Switzerland
Answer: D
Fact: Tim Berners-Lee invented the Web in 1989 while working at CERN in Geneva.

## E6
Q: Who invented the vaccine, introducing the world's first one against smallpox?
A) Pasteur
B) Fleming
C) Jenner
D) Lister
Answer: C
Fact: Edward Jenner created the first smallpox vaccine in 1796 using cowpox, laying the foundation for all modern immunology.

## E7
Q: What nationality was Karl Benz, inventor of the petrol-powered automobile?
A) French
B) British
C) German
D) Italian
Answer: C
Fact: Benz's wife Bertha secretly took the car on a 104 km road trip in 1888 to prove its practical use.

## E8
Q: In which century was photography publicly introduced?
A) 17th
B) 18th
C) 19th
D) 20th
Answer: C
Fact: Louis Daguerre's daguerreotype process was announced to the world in 1839.

## E9
Q: Who invented the sewing machine?
A) Singer
B) Howe
C) Whitney
D) Fulton
Answer: B
Fact: Elias Howe patented his lock-stitch sewing machine in 1846; Isaac Singer later popularised a rival design.

## E10
Q: Who improved the steam engine with a separate condenser, powering the Industrial Revolution?
A) Brunel
B) Stephenson
C) Watt
D) Arkwright
Answer: C
Fact: James Watt's improvements made steam engines practical for factories and gave us the unit of power called the watt.

## M1
Q: Which American company released the first mass-market personal computer, the Apple II, in 1977?
A) IBM
B) Xerox
C) Apple
D) Commodore
Answer: C
Fact: The Apple II was one of the first PCs to include colour graphics and an open architecture for expansion cards.

## M2
Q: In which country was aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid) first synthesised commercially?
A) Britain
B) France
C) Germany
D) USA
Answer: C
Fact: Felix Hoffmann at Bayer synthesised a stable form of aspirin in 1897, and Bayer trademarked the name.

## M3
Q: Samuel Morse co-invented the telegraph — which country did he come from?
A) Canada
B) Britain
C) USA
D) France
Answer: C
Fact: Morse demonstrated his electric telegraph to the US Congress in 1844 with the message 'What hath God wrought'.

## M4
Q: Who invented the first refracting telescope in 1608?
A) Galileo
B) Huygens
C) Lippershey
D) Newton
Answer: C
Fact: Hans Lippershey, a Dutch spectacle-maker, filed the earliest known patent for a telescope.

## M5
Q: Who invented Bakelite, the first fully synthetic plastic?
A) Chardonnet
B) Baekeland
C) Carothers
D) Parkes
Answer: B
Fact: Leo Baekeland patented Bakelite in 1909; the name is simply derived from his own surname.

## M6
Q: Cyrus McCormick invented the mechanical grain reaper in 1831 — from which country?
A) Canada
B) Britain
C) USA
D) Australia
Answer: C
Fact: McCormick's reaper could harvest as much grain in a day as five workers using hand scythes.

## M7
Q: Who invented the hovercraft in 1959?
A) Barnes Wallis
B) Cockerell
C) Whittle
D) de Havilland
Answer: B
Fact: Christopher Cockerell tested his first hovercraft concept using two tin cans and a vacuum cleaner.

## M8
Q: Alessandro Volta invented the electric battery — what was his nationality?
A) Spanish
B) French
C) Italian
D) German
Answer: C
Fact: Napoleon was so impressed by Volta's voltaic pile that he made him a count and a senator.

## M9
Q: Who invented the process of pasteurisation?
A) Koch
B) Lister
C) Pasteur
D) Jenner
Answer: C
Fact: Louis Pasteur developed pasteurisation in 1864 originally to stop wine and beer from spoiling.

## M10
Q: In which decade was nylon first commercially sold?
A) 1920s
B) 1930s
C) 1940s
D) 1950s
Answer: B
Fact: DuPont's Wallace Carothers invented nylon; nylon stockings sold out within hours at their 1939 launch.

## H1
Q: Which inventor built the Difference Engine, an early mechanical computer?
A) Lovelace
B) Turing
C) Babbage
D) Hollerith
Answer: C
Fact: Charles Babbage designed the Difference Engine in 1822 but lacked the funding and precision engineering to complete it.

## H2
Q: Nikola Tesla was born in Smiljan, a village now located in which modern country?
A) Austria
B) Hungary
C) Serbia
D) Croatia
Answer: D
Fact: Tesla was ethnically Serbian and born in 1856 in Smiljan, which was then part of the Austrian Empire and is now in Croatia.

## H3
Q: Who invented the bifocal lens?
A) Franklin
B) Huygens
C) Hooke
D) Rumford
Answer: A
Fact: Benjamin Franklin designed bifocals around 1784 so he could see both near and far without switching spectacles.

## H4
Q: Who invented the process of vulcanising rubber?
A) Dunlop
B) Goodyear
C) Michelin
D) Hancock
Answer: B
Fact: Charles Goodyear discovered vulcanisation accidentally in 1839 when he dropped rubber mixed with sulphur onto a hot stove.

## H5
Q: Wilhelm Röntgen discovered X-rays in 1895 — what was his nationality?
A) Austrian
B) Swiss
C) Dutch
D) German
Answer: D
Fact: Röntgen won the very first Nobel Prize in Physics in 1901 for his discovery of X-rays.

## H6
Q: Who invented the first commercially successful punched-card tabulating machine, used in the 1890 US Census?
A) Hollerith
B) Jacquard
C) Babbage
D) Burroughs
Answer: A
Fact: Herman Hollerith's company later merged with others to become IBM in 1911.

## H7
Q: Who invented the Haber-Bosch process for synthesising ammonia?
A) Bosch
B) Haber
C) Ostwald
D) Bunsen
Answer: B
Fact: Fritz Haber's nitrogen-fixation process enabled mass fertiliser production and now feeds roughly half the world's population.

## H8
Q: In which decade did John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, and William Shockley invent the transistor?
A) 1930s
B) 1940s
C) 1950s
D) 1960s
Answer: B
Fact: The transistor was invented at Bell Labs in 1947 and went on to become the fundamental building block of modern electronics.

## H9
Q: Who invented the jet engine independently in Britain during the 1930s?
A) Cayley
B) Whittle
C) de Havilland
D) Sopwith
Answer: B
Fact: Frank Whittle patented his turbojet design in 1930, but the RAF initially showed little interest and funding was delayed for years.

## H10
Q: Édouard Branly invented the coherer, a key component for early radio reception — what was his nationality?
A) Belgian
B) Italian
C) French
D) Russian
Answer: C
Fact: Branly's coherer, developed in 1890, allowed radio waves to be detected and was used in Marconi's early wireless experiments.
