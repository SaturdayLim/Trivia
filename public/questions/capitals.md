# Category: Capitals

## E1
Q: What is the capital city of France?
A) Lyon
B) Paris
C) Marseille
D) Bordeaux
Answer: B
Fact: Paris is known as the 'City of Light', a nickname earned partly from its early adoption of gas street lighting in the 1800s.

## E2
Q: Which city is nicknamed 'The Big Apple'?
A) Chicago
B) Los Angeles
C) New York City
D) Miami
Answer: C
Fact: The 'Big Apple' nickname was popularised in the 1970s by a tourism campaign to boost New York's image during a fiscal crisis.

## E3
Q: What is the capital of Japan?
A) Osaka
B) Kyoto
C) Hiroshima
D) Tokyo
Answer: D
Fact: Tokyo was originally a small fishing village called Edo before becoming Japan's capital and one of the world's most populous cities.

## E4
Q: Which city is the capital of Egypt?
A) Alexandria
B) Luxor
C) Cairo
D) Aswan
Answer: C
Fact: Cairo is the largest city in Africa and the Arab world, and sits just kilometres from the ancient Pyramids of Giza.

## E5
Q: What is the capital city of Brazil?
A) Rio de Janeiro
B) Sao Paulo
C) Brasilia
D) Salvador
Answer: C
Fact: Brasilia was purpose-built as Brazil's capital and was constructed in just 41 months, opening in 1960.

## E6
Q: Which city is the capital of Spain?
A) Barcelona
B) Seville
C) Valencia
D) Madrid
Answer: D
Fact: Madrid is the highest-altitude capital city in the European Union, sitting about 667 metres above sea level.

## E7
Q: Which city is called 'The Eternal City'?
A) Athens
B) Rome
C) Cairo
D) Jerusalem
Answer: B
Fact: Rome earned its 'Eternal City' nickname from ancient Romans who believed their city would last forever, and the phrase appears in writings as early as 25 BC.

## E8
Q: What is the capital of Australia?
A) Sydney
B) Melbourne
C) Brisbane
D) Canberra
Answer: D
Fact: Canberra was chosen as a compromise capital because Sydney and Melbourne both wanted the honour — it sits roughly halfway between them.

## E9
Q: What is the capital of Canada?
A) Toronto
B) Vancouver
C) Ottawa
D) Montreal
Answer: C
Fact: Ottawa was selected as Canada's capital by Queen Victoria in 1857, partly because its inland location made it harder for the United States to attack.

## E10
Q: What is the capital of China?
A) Shanghai
B) Beijing
C) Hong Kong
D) Guangzhou
Answer: B
Fact: Beijing has served as China's capital for more than 700 years and its name literally means 'Northern Capital' in Mandarin.

## M1
Q: Which city is the constitutional capital of the Netherlands?
A) Rotterdam
B) Utrecht
C) The Hague
D) Amsterdam
Answer: D
Fact: Although Amsterdam is the constitutional capital, the Dutch government and parliament are actually based in The Hague, not Amsterdam.

## M2
Q: Which city is nicknamed 'The Windy City'?
A) Boston
B) Dallas
C) Chicago
D) Denver
Answer: C
Fact: Despite the nickname, Chicago is not even in the top 10 windiest US cities — the label may have come from boastful politicians, not the weather.

## M3
Q: Which city is South Africa's legislative capital?
A) Johannesburg
B) Pretoria
C) Cape Town
D) Durban
Answer: C
Fact: South Africa has three capitals: Cape Town (legislative), Pretoria (executive), and Bloemfontein (judicial).

## M4
Q: In which country is the city of Istanbul?
A) Greece
B) Bulgaria
C) Turkey
D) Egypt
Answer: C
Fact: Istanbul is the world's only city that spans two continents — its European and Asian sides are separated by the Bosphorus strait.

## M5
Q: What is the capital of South Korea?
A) Busan
B) Incheon
C) Daegu
D) Seoul
Answer: D
Fact: Seoul has been the capital of Korea for over 600 years, founded as the capital of the Joseon dynasty in 1394.

## M6
Q: What is the capital of Argentina?
A) Cordoba
B) Rosario
C) Mendoza
D) Buenos Aires
Answer: D
Fact: Buenos Aires means 'fair winds' in Spanish, a name given by early Spanish sailors who found smooth sailing in the Rio de la Plata estuary.

## M7
Q: Which city is known as 'The City of Canals'?
A) Bruges
B) Amsterdam
C) Venice
D) Bangkok
Answer: C
Fact: Venice is built across 118 small islands connected by over 400 bridges, with canals serving as its main roads.

## M8
Q: What is the capital of Sweden?
A) Gothenburg
B) Malmo
C) Uppsala
D) Stockholm
Answer: D
Fact: Stockholm is built across 14 islands where Lake Malaren meets the Baltic Sea, earning it the nickname 'Venice of the North'.

## M9
Q: What is the capital of Nigeria?
A) Lagos
B) Kano
C) Ibadan
D) Abuja
Answer: D
Fact: Nigeria moved its capital from Lagos to Abuja in 1991 to have a more centrally located capital free from Lagos's overcrowding.

## M10
Q: In which country is the city of Casablanca?
A) Algeria
B) Tunisia
C) Morocco
D) Libya
Answer: C
Fact: Despite its fame, Casablanca is not Morocco's capital — Rabat holds that title, though Casablanca is the country's largest and most economically important city.

## H1
Q: What is the capital of Kazakhstan?
A) Almaty
B) Shymkent
C) Astana
D) Karaganda
Answer: C
Fact: Kazakhstan's capital has changed names multiple times — it was Akmola, then Astana, then briefly Nur-Sultan, and returned to Astana in 2022.

## H2
Q: What is the capital of Eritrea?
A) Dire Dawa
B) Djibouti
C) Massawa
D) Asmara
Answer: D
Fact: Asmara is famous for its remarkably well-preserved Italian modernist and Art Deco architecture, earning it UNESCO World Heritage status in 2017.

## H3
Q: Which city's name literally means 'Lion City' in Sanskrit?
A) Bangkok
B) Colombo
C) Kuala Lumpur
D) Singapore
Answer: D
Fact: Singapore derives from the Sanskrit 'Singapura' (singa = lion, pura = city), though no lions are native to the island — the founder may have seen a tiger and mistaken it.

## H4
Q: What is the capital of Kosovo?
A) Mitrovica
B) Peja
C) Pristina
D) Prizren
Answer: C
Fact: Kosovo declared independence from Serbia in 2008 and is one of the world's newest countries, though its independence remains disputed by some nations.

## H5
Q: Which city is Bolivia's constitutional capital?
A) La Paz
B) Santa Cruz
C) Cochabamba
D) Sucre
Answer: D
Fact: Bolivia has two capitals — Sucre is the constitutional capital and seat of the judiciary, while La Paz is the seat of government and legislative capital.

## H6
Q: In which country is the city of Tirana?
A) Serbia
B) Albania
C) North Macedonia
D) Montenegro
Answer: B
Fact: Tirana became Albania's capital in 1920 and has transformed dramatically since the fall of communism in 1991, with colourful buildings painted by a former mayor-artist.

## H7
Q: What is the capital of Bhutan?
A) Punakha
B) Paro
C) Phuentsholing
D) Thimphu
Answer: D
Fact: Thimphu is one of only two Asian capital cities without a traffic light system — police officers direct traffic at the main intersection instead.

## H8
Q: What is the capital of Burkina Faso?
A) Bobo-Dioulasso
B) Ouagadougou
C) Bamako
D) Niamey
Answer: B
Fact: Ouagadougou's name comes from the Moore language meaning 'you are welcome here at home with us', and the city is affectionately nicknamed 'Ouaga' by locals.

## H9
Q: Which ancient city is called 'The Rose-Red City'?
A) Marrakech
B) Sanaa
C) Petra
D) Muscat
Answer: C
Fact: Petra in Jordan earned the nickname 'Rose-Red City' from a poem by John William Burgon describing its rock-carved architecture glowing pink in the desert sun.

## H10
Q: What is the capital of the Federated States of Micronesia?
A) Majuro
B) Koror
C) Palikir
D) Tarawa
Answer: C
Fact: Palikir is one of the world's smallest capitals by population, with only a few thousand residents in this tiny Pacific island nation's government town.
