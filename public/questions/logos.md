# Category: Logos

## E1
Q: Which brand uses the slogan 'Just Do It'?
A) Adidas
B) Nike
C) Puma
D) Reebok
Answer: B
Fact: Nike's 'Just Do It' slogan was inspired by the last words of a convicted murderer before his execution in 1977.

## E2
Q: What fruit appears on Apple's iconic logo?
A) Pear
B) Plum
C) Apple
D) Peach
Answer: C
Fact: The Apple logo has a bite taken out of it, which some say is a play on the word 'byte' — a unit of digital information.

## E3
Q: What is the primary colour of the McDonald's logo?
A) Red
B) Orange
C) Yellow
D) Green
Answer: C
Fact: McDonald's famous 'Golden Arches' were originally the shape of two separate arches on either side of early restaurant buildings.

## E4
Q: Which brand's slogan is 'I'm Lovin' It'?
A) Burger King
B) KFC
C) Subway
D) McDonald's
Answer: D
Fact: The 'I'm Lovin' It' jingle was written by Pharrell Williams and has been used globally since 2003.

## E5
Q: Which fast food chain's mascot is Colonel Sanders?
A) Popeyes
B) Chick-fil-A
C) KFC
D) Church's
Answer: C
Fact: Colonel Harland Sanders was a real person who founded KFC and continued as its brand ambassador until his death in 1980.

## E6
Q: What colour is the Coca-Cola logo?
A) Blue
B) Red
C) Black
D) Green
Answer: B
Fact: Coca-Cola's red colour is so associated with Christmas that many people believe the brand invented Santa's red suit, though that is a myth.

## E7
Q: What animal features in the Lacoste logo?
A) Alligator
B) Lizard
C) Crocodile
D) Dragon
Answer: C
Fact: Founder René Lacoste earned the nickname 'The Crocodile' from American press due to his tenacity on the tennis court.

## E8
Q: Which brand uses the slogan 'Think Different'?
A) Microsoft
B) Google
C) Apple
D) Samsung
Answer: C
Fact: 'Think Different' was created by TBWA/Chiat/Day in 1997, the same year Steve Jobs returned to Apple.

## E9
Q: What colour is the Starbucks logo?
A) Blue
B) Green
C) Brown
D) Black
Answer: B
Fact: The Starbucks siren is based on a 16th-century Norse woodcut of a twin-tailed mermaid, chosen to reflect Seattle's maritime heritage.

## E10
Q: Which brand uses the slogan 'Because You're Worth It'?
A) Chanel
B) Revlon
C) Maybelline
D) L'Oreal
Answer: D
Fact: The slogan was created in 1973 by a young female copywriter — one of the first ads to speak directly to women's self-worth rather than pleasing others.

## M1
Q: What shape forms the central symbol in the Mercedes-Benz logo?
A) Four-pointed star
B) Three-pointed star
C) Five-pointed star
D) Six-pointed star
Answer: B
Fact: The three-pointed Mercedes star represents the company's ambition to dominate land, sea, and air transportation.

## M2
Q: What is the dominant colour of the Pepsi logo?
A) Red
B) Blue
C) Green
D) Purple
Answer: B
Fact: Pepsi adopted blue as its primary brand colour in the 1950s partly to differentiate itself more clearly from Coca-Cola's red.

## M3
Q: Which car brand's slogan is 'The Ultimate Driving Machine'?
A) Audi
B) Porsche
C) BMW
D) Lexus
Answer: C
Fact: 'The Ultimate Driving Machine' has been used by BMW in North America since 1973 and is one of the longest-running automotive taglines.

## M4
Q: What is the name of the Michelin tyre mascot?
A) Goodyear
B) Bibendum
C) Dunlop
D) Radial
Answer: B
Fact: Bibendum, the Michelin Man, was created in 1898 and is one of the world's oldest trademarks still in use.

## M5
Q: What animal leaps across the Puma logo?
A) Lion
B) Cheetah
C) Puma
D) Panther
Answer: C
Fact: Puma and Adidas were both founded by brothers — Rudolf and Adolf Dassler — who had a famous falling-out after World War II.

## M6
Q: Which brand's slogan is 'Connecting People'?
A) Motorola
B) Sony
C) Nokia
D) Ericsson
Answer: C
Fact: Nokia's 'Connecting People' slogan has been used since 1992 and helped position the company as more than just a technology firm.

## M7
Q: How many colours are in the Google wordmark logo?
A) Two
B) Three
C) Four
D) Five
Answer: C
Fact: Google uses red, blue, yellow, and green in its logo — deliberately breaking the pattern by colouring the second 'g' green instead of following the primary-colour sequence.

## M8
Q: Which sportswear brand's logo features three parallel stripes?
A) Nike
B) Puma
C) Adidas
D) Reebok
Answer: C
Fact: Adidas purchased the rights to its iconic three-stripe design from Finnish company Karhu Sports in 1952 for two bottles of whisky and the equivalent of roughly 1,600 euros.

## M9
Q: Which breakfast cereal mascot says 'They're Gr-r-reat!'?
A) Chester Cheetah
B) Toucan Sam
C) Tony Tiger
D) Snap Crackle
Answer: C
Fact: Tony the Tiger has been the mascot for Kellogg's Frosted Flakes since 1952, making his catchphrase one of the most recognised in advertising history.

## M10
Q: Which confectionery brand says 'Taste the Rainbow'?
A) M&Ms
B) Skittles
C) Starburst
D) Jolly Rancher
Answer: B
Fact: 'Taste the Rainbow' has been Skittles' slogan since 1994 and is one of the most recognised confectionery taglines globally.

## H1
Q: What is the official name of Tiffany and Co.'s signature packaging colour?
A) Aqua Blue
B) Turquoise
C) Tiffany Blue
D) Cerulean
Answer: C
Fact: Tiffany Blue is formally Pantone 1837 — the number referencing 1837, the year Charles Lewis Tiffany founded the company.

## H2
Q: How many stars appear in the Subaru logo?
A) Five
B) Six
C) Seven
D) Eight
Answer: B
Fact: The Subaru logo depicts the Pleiades star cluster; 'Subaru' is the Japanese word for Pleiades, and the six stars represent five companies that merged to form Fuji Heavy Industries.

## H3
Q: Which brand's slogan is 'The Quint-essential Swiss Watch'?
A) Rolex
B) Patek Philippe
C) IWC
D) Breguet
Answer: C
Fact: IWC Schaffhausen is the only major Swiss watch manufacturer based in the German-speaking part of Switzerland, a fact highlighted by this slogan.

## H4
Q: What bird appears in the Hollister Co. logo?
A) Eagle
B) Hawk
C) Seagull
D) Pelican
Answer: C
Fact: Hollister's seagull logo evokes a Southern California surf lifestyle, despite the brand being owned by Ohio-based Abercrombie and Fitch.

## H5
Q: How many interlocking rings are in the Audi logo?
A) Three
B) Four
C) Five
D) Six
Answer: B
Fact: Audi's four rings represent the four founding companies of Auto Union — Audi, DKW, Horch, and Wanderer — which merged in 1932.

## H6
Q: What animal is the Aflac insurance brand mascot?
A) Goose
B) Duck
C) Swan
D) Pelican
Answer: B
Fact: The Aflac Duck debuted in 2000 and is credited with boosting Aflac's brand recognition in the US from 12% to over 90% within just a few years.

## H7
Q: What colour is the UPS shield logo?
A) Black
B) Dark Brown
C) Bronze
D) Maroon
Answer: B
Fact: UPS trademarked its specific shade called 'Pullman Brown,' chosen in 1916 because dirt and scuffs were less visible on brown vehicles.

## H8
Q: In the Amazon logo, the arrow-smile stretches from the letter A to which other letter?
A) M
B) N
C) Z
D) O
Answer: C
Fact: The Amazon smile-arrow from A to Z represents that the company sells everything from A to Z, and that deliveries bring smiles to customers.

## H9
Q: What prize did the designer of the Planters Mr. Peanut mascot win in 1916?
A) $5
B) $10
C) $25
D) $50
Answer: A
Fact: Antonio Gentile was just 14 years old when he won the $5 contest prize for drawing Mr. Peanut, one of America's longest-running brand characters.

## H10
Q: Which brand's famous slogan is 'Diamonds Are Forever'?
A) Tiffany
B) Cartier
C) De Beers
D) Swarovski
Answer: C
Fact: Coined in 1947 by copywriter Frances Gerety, 'Diamonds Are Forever' was voted the top advertising slogan of the 20th century by Advertising Age magazine.
