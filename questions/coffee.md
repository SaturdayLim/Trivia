# Category: Coffee

## E1
Q: What coffee drink is made with espresso and steamed milk foam?
A) Cappuccino
B) Americano
C) Cold Brew
D) Ristretto
Answer: A
Fact: A classic cappuccino is traditionally one-third espresso, one-third steamed milk, and one-third foam.

## E2
Q: Which drink is simply espresso diluted with hot water?
A) Flat White
B) Macchiato
C) Americano
D) Lungo
Answer: C
Fact: The Americano is said to have been invented when American soldiers in WWII diluted Italian espresso to mimic drip coffee.

## E3
Q: Which country is widely considered the birthplace of coffee?
A) Brazil
B) Colombia
C) Ethiopia
D) Vietnam
Answer: C
Fact: Legend says an Ethiopian goat herder named Kaldi first noticed the energising effect of coffee berries around 850 AD.

## E4
Q: What colour are raw, unroasted coffee beans?
A) Brown
B) Black
C) Green
D) Yellow
Answer: C
Fact: Green coffee beans still contain chlorophyll; the familiar brown colour only develops during the roasting process.

## E5
Q: A latte is espresso combined with what?
A) Steamed milk
B) Cold cream
C) Hot water
D) Condensed milk
Answer: A
Fact: The word latte is Italian for milk, and a proper latte has far more steamed milk than a cappuccino.

## E6
Q: What does barista mean in Italian?
A) Chef
B) Bartender
C) Roaster
D) Grinder
Answer: B
Fact: In Italy, a barista serves all kinds of drinks at a bar, not just coffee — the coffee-only meaning is largely an English-world adoption.

## E7
Q: Vietnamese iced coffee is made with sweetened condensed milk and what type of coffee filter?
A) Chemex
B) Phin
C) AeroPress
D) Moka Pot
Answer: B
Fact: The phin is a small metal drip filter that brews coffee slowly by gravity, producing the thick, strong brew used in Vietnamese iced coffee.

## E8
Q: Which machine forces hot water through ground coffee under pressure?
A) French Press
B) Percolator
C) Espresso Machine
D) Chemex
Answer: C
Fact: The first commercial espresso machine was patented in Italy by Angelo Moriondo in 1884.

## E9
Q: Which of these is a major species of coffee bean?
A) Arabica
B) Valdiva
C) Sumatra
D) Harrar
Answer: A
Fact: Arabica beans account for around 60 to 70 percent of global coffee production and are generally considered smoother and more aromatic than Robusta.

## E10
Q: A mocha coffee drink combines espresso, milk, and which flavouring?
A) Vanilla
B) Caramel
C) Chocolate
D) Hazelnut
Answer: C
Fact: The mocha is named after the Yemeni port city of Mocha, which was a major coffee trading hub for centuries.

## M1
Q: Which brewing method uses a cloth or paper filter and a controlled slow pour?
A) Moka Pot
B) Pour Over
C) French Press
D) Siphon
Answer: B
Fact: The Melitta pour-over was invented by Melitta Bentz in Germany in 1908 using blotting paper from her son's notebook.

## M2
Q: Which country is the world's largest coffee producer?
A) Colombia
B) Vietnam
C) Brazil
D) Indonesia
Answer: C
Fact: Brazil has been the world's top coffee producer for over 150 years, growing mainly Arabica but also significant Robusta.

## M3
Q: Compared to a latte, a flat white has what difference?
A) More foam
B) Less milk
C) No espresso
D) Iced only
Answer: B
Fact: A flat white uses a smaller milk-to-espresso ratio than a latte, resulting in a stronger coffee flavour in a smaller cup.

## M4
Q: Which roast level is typically lightest in colour?
A) French Roast
B) City Roast
C) Light Roast
D) Full City
Answer: C
Fact: Light roast beans are roasted to just before or at the first crack, preserving more of the bean's original fruity and floral notes.

## M5
Q: What is the golden foam layer on top of a freshly pulled espresso called?
A) Froth
B) Crema
C) Bloom
D) Scum
Answer: B
Fact: Crema forms when CO2 dissolved in the bean is rapidly released during extraction, creating the signature reddish-golden layer on espresso.

## M6
Q: What is a Moka Pot also commonly called?
A) Stovetop Espresso
B) Drip Brewer
C) Cold Dripper
D) Vacuum Pot
Answer: A
Fact: The Moka pot was invented by Alfonso Bialetti in 1933 and is still manufactured by the Bialetti company today.

## M7
Q: Yirgacheffe, a celebrated coffee growing region, is located in which country?
A) Kenya
B) Ethiopia
C) Guatemala
D) Yemen
Answer: B
Fact: Yirgacheffe coffees are celebrated worldwide for their bright floral aromas and notes of bergamot, jasmine, and citrus.

## M8
Q: What does ristretto mean in Italian?
A) Lungo
B) Restricted
C) Double
D) Strong
Answer: B
Fact: A ristretto uses the same coffee dose as espresso but half the water, producing an even more concentrated and sweeter shot.

## M9
Q: Dalgona coffee — whipped instant coffee over cold milk — went viral in which year?
A) 2010
B) 2015
C) 2020
D) 2018
Answer: C
Fact: Dalgona coffee went viral globally during early pandemic lockdowns, with millions recreating it at home on social media.

## M10
Q: Coffee spread into Europe largely through trade with which empire?
A) Persian
B) Mongol
C) Ottoman
D) Mughal
Answer: C
Fact: Istanbul's first coffeehouses opened in the 1550s, and Ottoman merchants and diplomats helped bring coffee culture to Vienna and beyond.

## H1
Q: What is the processing method where coffee cherries ferment inside a sealed oxygen-free tank?
A) Washed
B) Natural
C) Anaerobic
D) Honey
Answer: C
Fact: Anaerobic fermentation creates unusual fruity, wine-like flavour profiles by limiting oxygen during the fermentation stage.

## H2
Q: The Geisha coffee variety was originally discovered in which country?
A) Japan
B) Panama
C) Ethiopia
D) Colombia
Answer: C
Fact: The Geisha variety was discovered in Ethiopia's Gori Gesha forest and later made world-famous by Panama's Hacienda La Esmeralda farm.

## H3
Q: What water temperature range is considered optimal for espresso extraction?
A) 75-80C
B) 85-89C
C) 90-96C
D) 98-100C
Answer: C
Fact: Water that is too hot over-extracts bitter compounds, while too-cool water under-extracts, leaving a sour and weak shot.

## H4
Q: Which coffee species has roughly double the caffeine content of Arabica?
A) Liberica
B) Excelsa
C) Robusta
D) Stenophylla
Answer: C
Fact: Robusta's higher caffeine content acts as a natural pesticide, making the plant hardier and easier to grow than Arabica.

## H5
Q: In coffee roasting, what causes the audible popping sound known as the first crack?
A) Bean splitting
B) Steam release
C) Caramelisation
D) Oil expansion
Answer: B
Fact: As moisture inside the bean turns to steam, pressure builds until the cell walls crack — a sound roasters use to gauge roast level.

## H6
Q: In speciality coffee, extraction yield measures what percentage of the bean?
A) Caffeine content
B) Dissolved solids
C) Bean moisture
D) Oil content
Answer: B
Fact: Speciality coffee baristas target an extraction yield of 18 to 22 percent, the share of the bean's mass that dissolves into the cup.

## H7
Q: Which Indonesian island's unique wet-hulling process produces its famous earthy, full-bodied coffee?
A) Bali
B) Java
C) Sumatra
D) Sulawesi
Answer: C
Fact: Sumatran coffees use the wet-hulling method called Giling Basah, which produces their distinctive low-acid, syrupy body and earthy flavour.

## H8
Q: What is the standard espresso brew ratio by weight?
A) 1:5
B) 1:2
C) 1:8
D) 1:15
Answer: B
Fact: A 1:2 ratio means 18g of ground coffee yields approximately 36g of espresso — the foundation of most modern espresso calibration.

## H9
Q: Which coffee processing method leaves fruit mucilage partially on the bean during drying?
A) Washed
B) Natural
C) Honey
D) Wet Hulling
Answer: C
Fact: The honey process originated in Costa Rica and produces coffees with sweetness sitting between washed clarity and natural fruitiness.

## H10
Q: Which city is recorded as opening Europe's first coffeehouse, around 1645?
A) Paris
B) London
C) Venice
D) Vienna
Answer: C
Fact: Venice's early coffeehouses appeared after trade with the Ottoman Empire brought coffee to Italian ports, predating London's famous coffee houses.
