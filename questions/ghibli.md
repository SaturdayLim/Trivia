# Category: Ghibli

## E1
Q: Which giant spirit becomes a bus in 'My Neighbour Totoro'?
A) Catbus
B) Kodama
C) No-Face
D) Jiji
Answer: A
Fact: The Catbus has twelve legs and its grin glows in the dark like a lantern.

## E2
Q: In 'Spirited Away', what is the name of the bathhouse where Chihiro works?
A) Yadoya
B) Aburaya
C) Yubaba
D) Kamaji
Answer: B
Fact: The Aburaya (oil shop) bathhouse was inspired by traditional Japanese onsen inns Miyazaki visited as a child.

## E3
Q: Which director co-founded Studio Ghibli and directed 'My Neighbour Totoro'?
A) Isao Takahata
B) Mamoru Hosoda
C) Makoto Shinkai
D) Hayao Miyazaki
Answer: D
Fact: Miyazaki reportedly had no confidence 'Totoro' would be popular and was surprised by its lasting success.

## E4
Q: What animal is Jiji, Kiki's companion in 'Kiki's Delivery Service'?
A) Rabbit
B) Crow
C) Cat
D) Fox
Answer: C
Fact: In the Japanese version Jiji never regains the ability to speak to Kiki at the film's end, symbolising growing up.

## E5
Q: Which Ghibli film features a young girl named San raised by wolves?
A) Nausicaa
B) Princess Mononoke
C) Spirited Away
D) Castle in the Sky
Answer: B
Fact: San's name literally means 'three' in Japanese, referencing her place as the third wolf cub.

## E6
Q: What is the name of the floating island in 'Castle in the Sky'?
A) Pazu
B) Laputa
C) Nausicaa
D) Totoro
Answer: B
Fact: Laputa takes its name from Jonathan Swift's fictional floating island in 'Gulliver's Travels'.

## E7
Q: What fire demon powers Howl's castle in 'Howl's Moving Castle'?
A) Haku
B) Calcifer
C) Turnip
D) Markl
Answer: B
Fact: Calcifer is a fallen star who made a contract with Howl, binding both of their lives together.

## E8
Q: In which film does a young boy named Sosuke befriend a goldfish princess?
A) Ponyo
B) Totoro
C) Arrietty
D) The Wind Rises
Answer: A
Fact: 'Ponyo' was inspired by Hans Christian Andersen's 'The Little Mermaid' but reimagined for a young child audience.

## E9
Q: What colour are the small Soot Sprites (susuwatari) in 'My Neighbour Totoro'?
A) White
B) Green
C) Black
D) Grey
Answer: C
Fact: Soot Sprites flee from light and dissolve into soot when they are touched by happiness.

## E10
Q: Which Ghibli film follows two siblings surviving alone in wartime Japan?
A) The Wind Rises
B) Pom Poko
C) Grave of the Fireflies
D) Only Yesterday
Answer: C
Fact: 'Grave of the Fireflies' was directed by Isao Takahata and is widely regarded as one of the most emotionally powerful war films ever made.

## M1
Q: What is the name of the witch who steals Chihiro's name in 'Spirited Away'?
A) Zeniba
B) Yubaba
C) Lin
D) Kamaji
Answer: B
Fact: Yubaba's twin sister Zeniba is her polar opposite — warm and kind despite having the same face.

## M2
Q: Who composed the iconic scores for most Studio Ghibli films?
A) Yoko Kanno
B) Toru Takemitsu
C) Joe Hisaishi
D) Shiro Sagisu
Answer: C
Fact: Joe Hisaishi has composed for every Miyazaki-directed Ghibli film since 'Nausicaa' in 1984.

## M3
Q: What is the name of Ashitaka's loyal animal companion in 'Princess Mononoke'?
A) Moro
B) Yakul
C) Okkoto
D) Kodama
Answer: B
Fact: Yakul is a red elk and one of the most loyal animal companions in any Ghibli film.

## M4
Q: Which Ghibli film is based on a novel by Diana Wynne Jones?
A) Kiki's Delivery Service
B) The Cat Returns
C) Howl's Moving Castle
D) Arrietty
Answer: C
Fact: Diana Wynne Jones was reportedly delighted by Miyazaki's adaptation despite its significant departures from her novel.

## M5
Q: What is the true name of the boy Chihiro meets, known as Haku, in 'Spirited Away'?
A) Ryujin
B) Kohaku
C) Kaonashi
D) Kamaji
Answer: B
Fact: Haku is the spirit of the Kohaku River, which was paved over — explaining why he forgot his own name.

## M6
Q: Miyazaki's visit to which region's mining communities inspired the working-class world of 'Castle in the Sky'?
A) Appalachia
B) South Wales
C) Silesia
D) Hokkaido
Answer: B
Fact: Miyazaki visited the South Wales valleys during a miners' strike in 1984, which deeply influenced the world of Pazu.

## M7
Q: Which Ghibli film follows a tanuki raccoon-dog clan defending their forest from developers?
A) Pom Poko
B) My Neighbours the Yamadas
C) Princess Kaguya
D) Nausicaa
Answer: A
Fact: In Japanese folklore, tanuki are famous shape-shifters — something 'Pom Poko' depicts in hilariously literal detail.

## M8
Q: What does No-Face offer the bathhouse workers in 'Spirited Away' to win their favour?
A) Sake
B) Spells
C) Gold
D) Food
Answer: C
Fact: No-Face can only mimic the greed and desires of whoever he absorbs — he has no true personality of his own.

## M9
Q: Which director helmed both 'The Tale of Princess Kaguya' and 'Grave of the Fireflies'?
A) Hiroyuki Morita
B) Yoshifumi Kondo
C) Isao Takahata
D) Goro Miyazaki
Answer: C
Fact: Takahata and Miyazaki were close collaborators for decades despite having very different directorial styles.

## M10
Q: Which Ghibli film is adapted from Mary Norton's novel 'The Borrowers'?
A) The Cat Returns
B) Arrietty
C) Porco Rosso
D) Ocean Waves
Answer: B
Fact: 'Arrietty' was the directorial debut of Hiromasa Yonebayashi, the youngest director in Ghibli's history at that time.

## H1
Q: What is the name of the six-armed boiler man who runs the bathhouse furnace in 'Spirited Away'?
A) Kamaji
B) Rin
C) Aniyaku
D) Boh
Answer: A
Fact: Kamaji's name translates roughly to 'boiler man' and his six arms let him operate all his controls simultaneously.

## H2
Q: Before Ghibli was founded, Miyazaki and Takahata worked together at which animation studio?
A) Mushi Production
B) Toei Animation
C) Madhouse
D) Sunrise
Answer: B
Fact: Miyazaki and Takahata first met at Toei Animation in the 1960s and led a historic labour union dispute there.

## H3
Q: What is the name of Sophie's younger sister in 'Howl's Moving Castle'?
A) Lettie
B) Martha
C) Honey
D) Fanny
Answer: A
Fact: In Diana Wynne Jones's novel there are three sisters — Lettie, Martha, and Sophie — each sent to a different apprenticeship.

## H4
Q: The bathhouse in 'Spirited Away' was most directly modelled on which type of traditional Japanese bathing establishment?
A) Ryokan
B) Sento
C) Izakaya
D) Onsen
Answer: B
Fact: A sento is a public neighbourhood bathhouse distinct from a private onsen — Miyazaki modelled the Aburaya on urban sento culture.

## H5
Q: Ghibli's first theatrical feature was an adaptation of Miyazaki's own manga. What was its title?
A) Castle in the Sky
B) Nausicaa
C) Future Boy Conan
D) Lupin III Cagliostro
Answer: B
Fact: 'Nausicaa of the Valley of the Wind' was produced in 1984 — technically before Ghibli was formally founded — and its commercial success led to Ghibli's creation.

## H6
Q: Which Ghibli film marked the directorial debut of Hayao Miyazaki's son, Goro Miyazaki?
A) The Wind Rises
B) Tales from Earthsea
C) From Up on Poppy Hill
D) The Cat Returns
Answer: B
Fact: Goro Miyazaki had no prior animation experience before 'Tales from Earthsea' — he had worked as a landscape architect.

## H7
Q: What is the name of the forest god whose severed head triggers disaster in 'Princess Mononoke'?
A) Shishigami
B) Kodama
C) Moro
D) Okkoto
Answer: A
Fact: The Shishigami (Deer God / Forest Spirit) transforms into the towering Nightwalker after sunset each night.

## H8
Q: Which Ghibli film features the song 'Toki ni wa Mukashi no Hanashi wo' performed by Tokiko Kato?
A) Nausicaa
B) Castle in the Sky
C) Porco Rosso
D) Only Yesterday
Answer: C
Fact: 'Toki ni wa Mukashi no Hanashi wo' was originally recorded by Tokiko Kato in 1971 and was a favourite of Miyazaki's long before 'Porco Rosso' was made.

## H9
Q: In 'The Tale of Princess Kaguya', from where is Kaguya said to have originally come?
A) The Sun
B) The Moon
C) The Sea
D) The Stars
Answer: B
Fact: The story is based on 'The Tale of the Bamboo Cutter', Japan's oldest known prose narrative, dating to the 10th century.

## H10
Q: Which character in 'Howl's Moving Castle' was invented entirely by Miyazaki with no equivalent in Diana Wynne Jones's novel?
A) Lettie
B) Calcifer
C) Madame Suliman
D) Turnip Head
Answer: C
Fact: Miyazaki merged and reimagined several of Jones's antagonists into the single character of Madame Suliman for narrative clarity.
