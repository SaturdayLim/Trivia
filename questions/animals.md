# Category: Animals

## E1
Q: What is a baby kangaroo called?
A) Cub
B) Joey
C) Pup
D) Kit
Answer: B
Fact: A newborn joey is only about the size of a jellybean when it first crawls into its mother's pouch.

## E2
Q: What is the fastest land animal?
A) Lion
B) Greyhound
C) Cheetah
D) Pronghorn
Answer: C
Fact: A cheetah can accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in just three seconds.

## E3
Q: A group of lions is called a what?
A) Pack
B) Herd
C) Pride
D) Flock
Answer: C
Fact: Most of the hunting in a pride is done by the lionesses, not the males.

## E4
Q: Which animal can regrow lost limbs?
A) Shark
B) Starfish
C) Crab
D) Jellyfish
Answer: B
Fact: Some starfish species can regrow an entirely new body from just a single severed arm.

## E5
Q: What type of animal is a Komodo dragon?
A) Snake
B) Crocodilian
C) Lizard
D) Turtle
Answer: C
Fact: The Komodo dragon is the world's largest living lizard, reaching up to 3 metres in length.

## E6
Q: Which is the largest animal on Earth?
A) Elephant
B) Blue Whale
C) Giraffe
D) Whale Shark
Answer: B
Fact: A blue whale's heart is so large a small child could crawl through its arteries.

## E7
Q: What is a baby cat called?
A) Pup
B) Cub
C) Kitten
D) Foal
Answer: C
Fact: Kittens are born with their eyes sealed shut and begin to open them after about 7 to 10 days.

## E8
Q: Which animal is known for playing dead to avoid predators?
A) Armadillo
B) Opossum
C) Hedgehog
D) Porcupine
Answer: B
Fact: Opossums don't consciously choose to play dead — it's an involuntary shock response that can last hours.

## E9
Q: What pigment gives flamingos their pink colour?
A) Carotenoids
B) Melanin
C) Chlorophyll
D) Keratin
Answer: A
Fact: Flamingos fed a diet without carotenoid pigments gradually turn white over time.

## E10
Q: What is a group of wolves called?
A) Pack
B) Mob
C) Gang
D) Tribe
Answer: A
Fact: Wolf packs typically consist of a breeding pair and their offspring from recent years.

## M1
Q: A group of crows is called a what?
A) Colony
B) Murder
C) Flock
D) Gaggle
Answer: B
Fact: Crows are among the most intelligent birds and have been observed using tools to obtain food.

## M2
Q: Which animal has the longest gestation period?
A) Elephant
B) Giraffe
C) Rhinoceros
D) Whale
Answer: A
Fact: African elephants carry their young for about 22 months — nearly two full years.

## M3
Q: What is a baby swan called?
A) Cygnet
B) Chick
C) Fledgling
D) Gosling
Answer: A
Fact: Young swans are known as cygnets and are typically grey-brown before moulting into white adult feathers.

## M4
Q: Which mammal is the only one capable of true sustained flight?
A) Flying Squirrel
B) Bat
C) Colugo
D) Sugar Glider
Answer: B
Fact: Bats make up about 20% of all classified mammal species in the world.

## M5
Q: What is a group of flamingos called?
A) Colony
B) Flamboyance
C) Flock
D) Bloom
Answer: B
Fact: Flamingos are highly social birds and colonies can number in the hundreds of thousands.

## M6
Q: Which bird can fly the highest, reaching altitudes above 11,000 metres?
A) Golden Eagle
B) Andean Condor
C) Bar-headed Goose
D) Whooper Swan
Answer: C
Fact: Bar-headed geese migrate directly over the Himalayas and have specially adapted haemoglobin to absorb oxygen at altitude.

## M7
Q: What is the only mammal with scales?
A) Armadillo
B) Pangolin
C) Echidna
D) Hedgehog
Answer: B
Fact: Pangolin scales are made of keratin — the same material as human fingernails.

## M8
Q: Which fish is a classic example of changing sex from female to male as it matures?
A) Clownfish
B) Wrasse
C) Salmon
D) Tuna
Answer: B
Fact: Many wrasse species are protogynous hermaphrodites, starting life as females before becoming dominant males.

## M9
Q: What is a baby goat called?
A) Foal
B) Lamb
C) Kid
D) Fawn
Answer: C
Fact: Goats and humans have shared a relationship for over 10,000 years, making them one of the earliest domesticated animals.

## M10
Q: What is the loudest animal on Earth relative to its body size?
A) Howler Monkey
B) Pistol Shrimp
C) Sperm Whale
D) Blue Whale
Answer: B
Fact: The pistol shrimp snaps its claw so fast it creates a cavitation bubble reaching temperatures hotter than the surface of the sun.

## H1
Q: A group of jellyfish is called a what?
A) Swarm
B) Smack
C) Bloom
D) Cloud
Answer: B
Fact: Jellyfish have no brain, heart, bones, or eyes, yet they have survived for over 500 million years.

## H2
Q: What is a baby echidna called?
A) Puggle
B) Joey
C) Hatchling
D) Pup
Answer: A
Fact: Echidnas are one of only five living monotreme species — egg-laying mammals that also nurse young with milk.

## H3
Q: Which animal has the most chromosomes?
A) Hermit Crab
B) Atlas Blue Butterfly
C) Stag Beetle
D) Crayfish
Answer: B
Fact: The Atlas blue butterfly holds the animal kingdom record with around 448 chromosomes compared to humans' 46.

## H4
Q: Which animal's blue blood is harvested to test vaccines and medical devices for contamination?
A) Squid
B) Horseshoe Crab
C) Sea Urchin
D) Mantis Shrimp
Answer: B
Fact: Horseshoe crab blood contains Limulus Amebocyte Lysate (LAL), the gold standard for detecting bacterial toxins in medicines.

## H5
Q: What is the scientific name for the snow leopard?
A) Panthera uncia
B) Panthera pardus
C) Neofelis nebulosa
D) Acinonyx jubatus
Answer: A
Fact: Snow leopards cannot roar — they are the only member of the Panthera genus without that ability.

## H6
Q: What is a group of ferrets called?
A) Colony
B) Gang
C) Business
D) Cluster
Answer: C
Fact: Ferrets were domesticated at least 2,500 years ago and were originally used to hunt rabbits and rodents.

## H7
Q: Which animal has the longest tongue relative to its body length?
A) Giant Anteater
B) Tube-lipped Nectar Bat
C) Chameleon
D) Sun Bear
Answer: B
Fact: The tube-lipped nectar bat's tongue is about 150% of its body length — the longest relative tongue of any mammal.

## H8
Q: Which animal can survive being completely frozen solid and thaw back to life?
A) Wood Frog
B) Arctic Tern
C) Tardigrade
D) Painted Turtle
Answer: A
Fact: The wood frog stops breathing and its heart stops beating when frozen, then fully revives each spring.

## H9
Q: What is a baby porcupine called?
A) Kit
B) Pup
C) Porcupette
D) Juvenile
Answer: C
Fact: Baby porcupines are born with soft quills that harden within hours of birth.

## H10
Q: The olm is a cave-dwelling salamander that can survive without food for how many years?
A) 2
B) 5
C) 10
D) 15
Answer: C
Fact: The olm, sometimes called the 'human fish', can live for over 100 years and is nearly completely blind.
