## E4
Q: What is the term used to describe food that complies with the dietary standards of traditional Jewish law?
A) Haram
B) Halal
C) Kosher
D) Vegan
Answer: C
Fact: Kosher food is specially slaughtered, inspected and prepared, making it more expensive. Some communities only eat meat from an animal that is certified "Glatt", meaning "smooth", where the animal's lungs were free of any adhesions.

## H3
Q: What type of pasta directly translates to "little tongues" in Italian?
A) Linguini
B) Fettuccine
C) Fusilli
D) Ravioli
Answer: A
Fact: Fettuccine means "small ribbon", Fusilli means "spindle" (traditionally produced by spinning dough around a spindle), and ravioli comes from "riavvolgere" meaning "to wrap".

## H4
Q: Fill in the blank: Bechamel, Veloute, Espagnole, Hollandaise and Tomato are known as the five ______ sauces.
A) Base
B) Mother
C) Root
D) Standard
Answer: B
Fact: These are used as starting points to making secondary sauces, likening each to the head of a matriarchal family. For example - Bechamel is used to make cream or cheese sauces, and Hollandaise is the base for Bearnaise or Dijon sauces.
