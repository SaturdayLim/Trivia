## H1
Q: Which of the following actually originated in France?
A) Baguette
B) Croissants
C) French Fries
D) Macaroons
Answer: A
Fact: Although the French made the Macaroon famous, it was brought over from Italy by nuns seeking refuge during the French Revolution. Macaroon was actually adapted from Macarone, which is Italian for "paste".
The Croissant originated in Austria as "Kipferl", a dense pastry that was brough over by an Austrian officer who opened a viennese bakery in Paris.
French fries, contrary to their name, is often known to originate in Belgium, and cut in the French style.
The baguette was a shape that cooked faster, an adaptation of Viennese-style baking to French labour laws that prohibited bakers from starting work before 4am.
