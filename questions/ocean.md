# Category: Ocean

## E1
Q: Which ocean is the largest on Earth?
A) Atlantic
B) Indian
C) Pacific
D) Arctic
Answer: C
Fact: The Pacific Ocean covers more area than all of Earth's landmasses combined.

## E2
Q: What is the largest fish in the ocean?
A) Great White Shark
B) Blue Marlin
C) Whale Shark
D) Giant Grouper
Answer: C
Fact: Whale sharks can reach lengths of over 12 metres and feed mainly on plankton.

## E3
Q: How many arms does a typical starfish have?
A) 4
B) 5
C) 6
D) 8
Answer: B
Fact: Some starfish species can regenerate a lost arm into an entirely new individual.

## E4
Q: What colour is the sea over most of Earth's surface?
A) Green
B) Turquoise
C) Blue
D) Grey
Answer: C
Fact: The ocean appears blue because water absorbs red wavelengths of sunlight and reflects blue ones.

## E5
Q: What is the world's largest coral reef system?
A) Mesoamerican Reef
B) Ningaloo Reef
C) Great Barrier Reef
D) Florida Reef
Answer: C
Fact: The Great Barrier Reef off Australia is so large it can be seen from outer space.

## E6
Q: Which is the largest animal ever known to exist?
A) Sperm Whale
B) Orca
C) Blue Whale
D) Humpback Whale
Answer: C
Fact: A blue whale's heart alone can weigh as much as a small car.

## E7
Q: What do we call the zone where the ocean meets the shore?
A) Abyssal
B) Intertidal
C) Hadal
D) Pelagic
Answer: B
Fact: Intertidal creatures must survive both submersion at high tide and exposure at low tide.

## E8
Q: What do dolphins use to navigate and hunt?
A) Echolocation
B) Magnetism
C) Electroreception
D) Eyesight
Answer: A
Fact: Dolphins can detect objects as small as a golf ball from over 70 metres away using echolocation.

## E9
Q: What primarily causes ocean tides on Earth?
A) Wind
B) Moon's gravity
C) Earth's rotation
D) Deep currents
Answer: B
Fact: The Sun also has a tidal effect on Earth's oceans, but it is about half as strong as the Moon's.

## E10
Q: Which ocean surrounds the North Pole?
A) Atlantic
B) Southern
C) Pacific
D) Arctic
Answer: D
Fact: The Arctic Ocean is the smallest and shallowest of Earth's five named oceans.

## M1
Q: What is the name of the deepest ocean trench on Earth?
A) Java Trench
B) Puerto Rico Trench
C) Mariana Trench
D) Tonga Trench
Answer: C
Fact: The deepest point in the Mariana Trench, Challenger Deep, reaches about 11,000 metres below sea level.

## M2
Q: What substance makes up the outer shell of a lobster?
A) Keratin
B) Calcium carbonate
C) Chitin
D) Silica
Answer: C
Fact: Lobsters must moult their chitinous shell to grow, leaving them briefly soft and vulnerable.

## M3
Q: What major ocean current keeps northern Europe warmer than expected?
A) California Current
B) Gulf Stream
C) Humboldt Current
D) Kuroshio Current
Answer: B
Fact: The Gulf Stream transports more water than all of Earth's rivers combined.

## M4
Q: What tiny animal actually builds coral reefs?
A) Krill
B) Sea Anemone
C) Coral Polyp
D) Barnacle
Answer: C
Fact: Each coral polyp secretes a calcium carbonate skeleton that accumulates over centuries to form reefs.

## M5
Q: What name is given to the open-ocean zone away from the seafloor?
A) Benthic
B) Hadal
C) Littoral
D) Pelagic
Answer: D
Fact: The pelagic zone is the largest habitat on Earth by volume and contains most of the ocean's water.

## M6
Q: Which sea is bordered by Europe, Africa, and Asia?
A) Red Sea
B) Caribbean Sea
C) Mediterranean Sea
D) Arabian Sea
Answer: C
Fact: The Mediterranean Sea has almost no tides because it is nearly enclosed with a narrow Atlantic opening.

## M7
Q: Which marine animal's blue blood is used in pharmaceutical safety testing?
A) Horseshoe Crab
B) Sea Turtle
C) Sea Cucumber
D) Mantis Shrimp
Answer: A
Fact: Horseshoe crab blood contains a compound used to test medical equipment for bacterial contamination.

## M8
Q: Below what depth does sunlight no longer penetrate the ocean?
A) 200 m
B) 500 m
C) 1,000 m
D) 2,000 m
Answer: A
Fact: Below 200 metres the ocean is called the twilight or mesopelagic zone, where photosynthesis cannot occur.

## M9
Q: What global ocean circulation pattern moves heat around the planet?
A) Trade Wind Drift
B) Thermohaline Circulation
C) Coriolis Loop
D) Ekman Spiral
Answer: B
Fact: Thermohaline circulation is driven by differences in water temperature and salinity, not wind.

## M10
Q: Approximately what percentage of seawater is salt?
A) 1.5%
B) 3.5%
C) 5%
D) 7%
Answer: B
Fact: If all the salt in the ocean were spread over land it would form a layer roughly 40 storeys tall.

## H1
Q: What is the name of the deepest point in the Atlantic Ocean?
A) Eustatia Deep
B) Milwaukee Deep
C) Romanche Deep
D) Vema Deep
Answer: B
Fact: Milwaukee Deep in the Puerto Rico Trench plunges to about 8,376 metres below sea level.

## H2
Q: What is the common name for Architeuthis dux?
A) Vampire Squid
B) Colossal Squid
C) Giant Squid
D) Humboldt Squid
Answer: C
Fact: Giant squids have the largest eyes of any known living animal, reaching up to 30 centimetres in diameter.

## H3
Q: Which cold current flows northward along South America's Pacific coast?
A) Benguela Current
B) Canary Current
C) Agulhas Current
D) Humboldt Current
Answer: D
Fact: The Humboldt Current supports one of the world's most productive marine ecosystems, rich in anchoveta.

## H4
Q: Which compound produces bioluminescence in most marine organisms?
A) Chlorophyll
B) Luciferin
C) Melanin
D) Carotenoid
Answer: B
Fact: Luciferin reacts with oxygen via the enzyme luciferase to produce cold, near-heatless light.

## H5
Q: What term describes the zone deeper than 6,000 m found only in trenches?
A) Abyssal
B) Bathyal
C) Hadal
D) Aphotic
Answer: C
Fact: The hadal zone is named after Hades, the Greek underworld, and harbours uniquely adapted amphipods.

## H6
Q: What event causes coral bleaching by expelling symbiotic algae?
A) Heat stress
B) Sediment smothering
C) Salinity drop
D) Storm damage
Answer: A
Fact: Corals expel their symbiotic zooxanthellae under heat stress, losing both their colour and their main food source.

## H7
Q: What type of bacteria forms the base of hydrothermal vent food chains?
A) Phototrophs
B) Chemolithotrophs
C) Heterotrophs
D) Methanogens
Answer: B
Fact: Chemolithotrophs harvest energy from hydrogen sulphide, powering entire vent ecosystems without sunlight.

## H8
Q: What is the world's saltiest open sea?
A) Caspian Sea
B) Caribbean Sea
C) Red Sea
D) Persian Gulf
Answer: C
Fact: The Red Sea's high salinity results from intense evaporation and very limited freshwater inflow from rivers.

## H9
Q: How many species of true seahorse (Hippocampus) are recognised by science?
A) 14
B) 27
C) 46
D) 62
Answer: C
Fact: Male seahorses carry and give birth to their young from a specialised brood pouch on their abdomen.

## H10
Q: Which ocean contains the longest mountain chain on Earth, the Mid-Ocean Ridge?
A) Indian
B) Southern
C) Arctic
D) Atlantic
Answer: D
Fact: The Mid-Atlantic Ridge runs the full length of the Atlantic Ocean and is where new seafloor is continuously created.
