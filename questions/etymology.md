# Category: Etymology

## E1
Q: The word 'volcano' comes from Vulcan, the Roman god of what?
A) War
B) Fire
C) Wind
D) Sea
Answer: B
Fact: Vulcan was the Roman blacksmith god associated with fire and the forge, giving us the word for fiery mountains.

## E2
Q: The word 'telephone' combines the Greek 'tele' (far) with 'phone', meaning what?
A) Sound
B) Light
C) Word
D) Signal
Answer: A
Fact: 'Phone' comes from the Greek 'phone', meaning voice or sound, which also gives us words like microphone and phonics.

## E3
Q: The word 'quiz' is believed to have originated in which country?
A) France
B) Ireland
C) America
D) Germany
Answer: B
Fact: The word 'quiz' likely originated in late 18th-century Dublin, though the exact story of its coining remains colourful and disputed.

## E4
Q: The word 'kangaroo' comes from which Indigenous Australian language group?
A) Warlpiri
B) Yolngu
C) Guugu Yimithirr
D) Aranda
Answer: C
Fact: When Captain Cook's crew asked locals what the animal was called, they replied 'gangurru' in the Guugu Yimithirr language of Queensland.

## E5
Q: The word 'brunch' is a portmanteau of breakfast and what other word?
A) Lunch
B) Munch
C) Crunch
D) Bunch
Answer: A
Fact: The word 'brunch' was first used in print in 1895 in a British magazine, cheerfully blending 'breakfast' and 'lunch' into one meal.

## E6
Q: The English word 'safari' is borrowed from which language?
A) Zulu
B) Swahili
C) Hausa
D) Amharic
Answer: B
Fact: The Swahili word 'safari' itself comes from the Arabic 'safar', meaning travel or journey, reflecting centuries of trade connections.

## E7
Q: The word 'democracy' combines the Greek 'demos' (people) with 'kratos', meaning what?
A) Freedom
B) Power
C) Vote
D) Rule
Answer: B
Fact: The same Greek root 'kratos' (power) also appears in 'bureaucracy' and 'aristocracy', all describing who holds authority.

## E8
Q: The name 'January' comes from which Roman god?
A) Jupiter
B) Janus
C) Julius
D) Jerome
Answer: B
Fact: Janus was the two-faced god of beginnings and doorways, making him a fitting namesake for the first month of the year.

## E9
Q: The word 'robot' was coined from Czech and originally meant what?
A) Machine
B) Worker
C) Servant
D) Steel
Answer: B
Fact: Czech writer Karel Capek introduced 'robot' in his 1920 play R.U.R., from the Czech word 'robota' meaning forced labour or drudgery.

## E10
Q: The word 'smog' is a portmanteau of smoke and what other word?
A) Fog
B) Hog
C) Log
D) Bog
Answer: A
Fact: The word 'smog' was coined around 1905 by Dr. Henry Antoine Des Voeux to describe London's infamous thick, polluted air.

## M1
Q: The word 'salary' derives from the Latin 'salarium', related to the Latin word for what?
A) Gold
B) Salt
C) Silver
D) Grain
Answer: B
Fact: Roman soldiers were sometimes paid in salt or given an allowance to buy it, giving rise to the phrase 'worth his salt'.

## M2
Q: The word 'assassin' is widely believed to have entered English via Arabic, where 'hashshashin' referred to users of what substance?
A) Opium
B) Poison
C) Hashish
D) Wine
Answer: C
Fact: The Arabic 'hashshashin' referred to followers of a medieval sect; the literal drug connection is colourful but historically debated.

## M3
Q: The English word 'algebra' is borrowed from Arabic and originally meant what?
A) Numbers
B) Reunion
C) Balance
D) Calculation
Answer: B
Fact: The Arabic 'al-jabr' means 'the reunion of broken parts', from the title of a 9th-century treatise by the mathematician al-Khwarizmi.

## M4
Q: The word 'Thursday' is named after which Norse god?
A) Odin
B) Loki
C) Freyr
D) Thor
Answer: D
Fact: The Old English name meant 'Thunder's Day', honouring Thor, the Norse god of thunder whose hammer also gave us 'thunderstruck'.

## M5
Q: The word 'awful' originally meant inspiring what kind of feeling?
A) Terror
B) Disgust
C) Awe
D) Pity
Answer: C
Fact: In Old English, 'aweful' meant 'full of reverential awe' — something deeply impressive — before gradually shifting to mean dreadful.

## M6
Q: The word 'motel' is a portmanteau of motor and what other word?
A) Hotel
B) Hostel
C) Home
D) Chateau
Answer: A
Fact: The first establishment to use the name 'motel' was the Milestone Mo-Tel in San Luis Obispo, California, opened in 1925.

## M7
Q: The English word 'bungalow' is a loanword from Hindi, originally referring to a house from which region?
A) Punjab
B) Bengal
C) Kashmir
D) Rajasthan
Answer: B
Fact: The Hindi 'bangla' means 'of Bengal', describing the low single-storey houses with wide verandas typical of that region.

## M8
Q: The word 'museum' derives from the Greek 'Mouseion', a temple dedicated to whom?
A) Athena
B) Apollo
C) The Muses
D) Zeus
Answer: C
Fact: The ancient Mouseion in Alexandria was a great centre of learning attached to the famous Library of Alexandria.

## M9
Q: The word 'nice' originally meant what in Middle English?
A) Kind
B) Foolish
C) Pleasant
D) Gentle
Answer: B
Fact: From Latin 'nescius' (ignorant), 'nice' spent centuries meaning silly or wanton before gradually softening to its modern meaning.

## M10
Q: The English word 'ketchup' is thought to have originated from a fish sauce word in which language?
A) Cantonese
B) Malay
C) Hokkien
D) Tagalog
Answer: C
Fact: The Hokkien word 'ke-tsiap' referred to a fermented fish sauce — tomatoes only entered the ketchup recipe much later in Western kitchens.

## H1
Q: The word 'companion' literally means one who shares what with you?
A) Water
B) Bread
C) Shelter
D) Wine
Answer: B
Fact: From Latin 'com-' (together) and 'panis' (bread) — a companion was literally someone you broke bread with at the same table.

## H2
Q: The word 'paradise' entered English from Old Persian, where it originally meant what?
A) Heaven
B) Garden
C) Afterlife
D) Bliss
Answer: B
Fact: Old Persian 'pairidaeza' meant a walled enclosed park or garden, used for royal hunting grounds before gaining its spiritual meaning.

## H3
Q: The English word 'shampoo' derives from the Hindi 'champo', which originally meant what?
A) Cleanse
B) Massage
C) Rinse
D) Lather
Answer: B
Fact: Early shampoo in 18th-century Britain was a head massage treatment, not a cleaning product — the hair-washing meaning came much later.

## H4
Q: The word 'hazard' came into English from Spanish, borrowed from Arabic 'az-zahr', meaning what?
A) Danger
B) Fate
C) Dice
D) Chance
Answer: C
Fact: The Arabic word for dice gave rise to a gambling game called 'hazard', and from the risks of gambling the word grew to mean any danger.

## H5
Q: Lewis Carroll coined 'chortle' in 'Jabberwocky' by blending 'chuckle' with what word?
A) Cackle
B) Snort
C) Gurgle
D) Guffaw
Answer: B
Fact: Carroll invented 'chortle' in his 1871 poem, and it has since become so widely used it is no longer considered a made-up nonce word.

## H6
Q: The word 'sinister' originally simply meant which direction in Latin?
A) Right
B) Left
C) North
D) Below
Answer: B
Fact: In Roman augury, omens seen on the left were considered unlucky, turning the neutral Latin word for 'left' into a word meaning evil or ominous.

## H7
Q: The word 'calculate' derives from the Latin 'calculus', meaning what small object?
A) Bead
B) Pebble
C) Shell
D) Chip
Answer: B
Fact: Romans used small pebbles on counting boards for arithmetic, so calculating literally meant moving pebbles — 'calculus' also gives us calcium.

## H8
Q: The English word 'avatar' comes from Sanskrit, where 'avatara' literally means what?
A) Image
B) Descent
C) Spirit
D) Form
Answer: B
Fact: In Hindu theology, an avatar is a deity 'descending' into earthly form — from 'ava' (down) and 'tarati' (he crosses), a divine crossing-down.

## H9
Q: Horace Walpole coined 'serendipity' in 1754, inspired by a fairy tale about princes from which place?
A) Persia
B) Ceylon
C) Arabia
D) India
Answer: B
Fact: Walpole read a tale called 'The Three Princes of Serendip' — Serendip being the old Persian name for the island now known as Sri Lanka.

## H10
Q: The word 'treacle' originally referred to an antidote against what in ancient Greek?
A) Poison
B) Plague
C) Wild beasts
D) Fevers
Answer: C
Fact: The Greek 'theriake' meant an antidote to bites from wild animals — over centuries it became a syrupy medicinal compound and then a sweet.
