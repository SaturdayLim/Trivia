# Category: Legends

## E1
Q: Which giant ape-like creature is said to roam the forests of North America?
A) Yeti
B) Bigfoot
C) Chupacabra
D) Wendigo
Answer: B
Fact: Bigfoot is also called Sasquatch, a name borrowed from a Salish Indigenous word meaning 'wild man'.

## E2
Q: In which Scottish lake does a famous sea monster supposedly live?
A) Loch Lomond
B) Loch Tay
C) Loch Ness
D) Loch Morar
Answer: C
Fact: The most famous 'Surgeon's Photograph' of Nessie taken in 1934 was later revealed to be a hoax using a toy submarine.

## E3
Q: Which blood-sucking creature from Latin American legend allegedly attacks livestock?
A) Wendigo
B) Chupacabra
C) Mothman
D) Skinwalker
Answer: B
Fact: The name 'Chupacabra' literally means 'goat-sucker' in Spanish, named for its alleged habit of draining goats of blood.

## E4
Q: How much of our brain do humans actually use, debunking the '10 percent myth'?
A) 50%
B) 75%
C) 100%
D) 25%
Answer: C
Fact: Brain scans show that virtually all areas of the brain are active at some point, and most are active nearly all the time.

## E5
Q: What is the Himalayan equivalent of Bigfoot called?
A) Yeti
B) Bunyip
C) Mapinguari
D) Almas
Answer: A
Fact: The Yeti is also known as the 'Abominable Snowman', a phrase that arose from a mistranslation of a Tibetan term in 1921.

## E6
Q: According to urban legend, what appears if you say 'Bloody Mary' three times in a dark mirror?
A) Ghost
B) Demon
C) Angel
D) Witch
Answer: A
Fact: The Bloody Mary legend may be linked to Queen Mary I of England, who was notorious for burning Protestant martyrs.

## E7
Q: What colour is the sun, contrary to the popular myth that it is yellow?
A) Orange
B) White
C) Yellow
D) Gold
Answer: B
Fact: The sun emits white light because it produces roughly equal amounts of every colour in the visible spectrum simultaneously.

## E8
Q: What does a child traditionally leave under a pillow for the Tooth Fairy?
A) Coin
B) Tooth
C) Feather
D) Bone
Answer: B
Fact: In many Spanish-speaking countries, a mouse called Ratoncito Perez plays the same role as the Tooth Fairy.

## E9
Q: What creature is said to haunt the Pine Barrens of New Jersey?
A) Mothman
B) Skinwalker
C) Jersey Devil
D) Wendigo
Answer: C
Fact: The Jersey Devil legend dates to 1735 and supposedly involves the cursed 13th child of a woman named Mother Leeds.

## E10
Q: According to a well-known myth, which animal buries its head in the sand when scared?
A) Emu
B) Flamingo
C) Ostrich
D) Pelican
Answer: C
Fact: Ostriches actually dig holes to tend their eggs and when they lower their heads into the holes, it can look like they are hiding.

## M1
Q: In the classic Lovers Lane urban legend, what did teenagers find dangling from the car door handle?
A) Knife
B) Hook
C) Chain
D) Noose
Answer: B
Fact: The Hook Man legend is thought to have originated in the 1950s as a cautionary tale warning teenagers away from parking at night.

## M2
Q: The Mothman was most famously sighted near which US town before a deadly bridge collapse?
A) Point Pleasant
B) Charleston
C) Huntington
D) Parkersburg
Answer: A
Fact: The Silver Bridge in Point Pleasant, West Virginia collapsed in 1967 killing 46 people, just weeks after Mothman sightings peaked.

## M3
Q: Which metal turns green naturally through oxidation, despite myths that it signals cheap quality?
A) Silver
B) Zinc
C) Copper
D) Tin
Answer: C
Fact: The green patina on copper is called verdigris and forms naturally through oxidation; even expensive copper roofs and statues turn green.

## M4
Q: In Slavic folklore, which witch lives in a hut on chicken legs deep in the forest?
A) Kikimora
B) Rusalka
C) Baba Yaga
D) Morozko
Answer: C
Fact: Baba Yaga can be either a villain or a helper depending on the story; her hut famously has no windows and turns to face visitors.

## M5
Q: Which US Founding Father invented the lightning rod, proving electricity in storms could be controlled?
A) Thomas Jefferson
B) George Washington
C) Benjamin Franklin
D) John Adams
Answer: C
Fact: Franklin's 1752 kite experiment proved lightning was electrical, and his rod invention saved countless buildings from fire.

## M6
Q: What unexpected creature does urban legend say lurks in the sewers beneath New York City?
A) Rats
B) Alligators
C) Crocodiles
D) Snakes
Answer: B
Fact: The legend likely grew from a 1935 New York Times story of a live alligator found in a Harlem storm drain by teenagers.

## M7
Q: Australia's bunyip cryptid is said to lurk in which type of habitat?
A) Desert
B) Mountains
C) Waterholes
D) Rainforest
Answer: C
Fact: The bunyip of Aboriginal Australian legend is said to guard swamps and billabongs and emit terrifying bellowing roars at night.

## M8
Q: What is the actual colour of blood inside the human body, debunking the 'blue vein' myth?
A) Blue
B) Purple
C) Red
D) Maroon
Answer: C
Fact: Blood is always red inside the body; veins appear blue through skin because of how different wavelengths of light penetrate tissue.

## M9
Q: In Irish folklore, which wailing spirit foretells the death of a family member?
A) Leprechaun
B) Banshee
C) Selkie
D) Clurichaun
Answer: B
Fact: The banshee (bean sidhe) is traditionally associated with certain old Irish families and her cry is called the 'caoine' or keening.

## M10
Q: In the 'Vanishing Hitchhiker' legend, the passenger who disappears mid-journey is always revealed to be what?
A) Ghost
B) Criminal
C) Twin
D) Angel
Answer: A
Fact: Versions of the Vanishing Hitchhiker legend appear in cultures worldwide, some dating back to horse-and-carriage era ghost stories.

## H1
Q: The Flatwoods Monster was reported by witnesses in 1952 in which US state?
A) Virginia
B) Kentucky
C) West Virginia
D) Ohio
Answer: C
Fact: Investigators later suggested the Flatwoods Monster was likely a barn owl perched on a tree, whose features were distorted by fear and darkness.

## H2
Q: In Filipino folklore, which creature is a flying upper torso that detaches from its legs at night to hunt?
A) Tikbalang
B) Manananggal
C) Aswang
D) Kapre
Answer: B
Fact: The Manananggal is said to prey on pregnant women and can be repelled by salt or ash sprinkled on its severed lower half.

## H3
Q: Napoleon Bonaparte's supposed short stature was partly a myth caused by confusion between French and which other measurement unit?
A) Roman inches
B) English inches
C) Metric metres
D) Dutch feet
Answer: B
Fact: Napoleon stood about 5 feet 7 inches (170 cm), average for a Frenchman of his time; the confusion arose between French and English inch units.

## H4
Q: The 'Slender Man' urban legend was first created on which online forum in 2009?
A) Reddit
B) 4chan
C) Something Awful
D) Creepypasta
Answer: C
Fact: Slender Man was created by user Eric Knudsen (known as 'Victor Surge') in a Photoshop contest on the Something Awful forums in 2009.

## H5
Q: The Ozark Howler, a large black cat cryptid, is named after which US highland region?
A) Appalachians
B) Ozarks
C) Smokies
D) Cascades
Answer: B
Fact: The Ozark Howler is often described as having glowing red eyes, and cryptozoologists debate whether it could be a misidentified puma.

## H6
Q: Scientists now recognise roughly how many human senses, far beyond the mythologised five?
A) 7
B) 15
C) 21
D) 33
Answer: C
Fact: Senses beyond the classic five include proprioception (body position), thermoception (temperature), nociception (pain), and vestibular sense (balance).

## H7
Q: The Brosno Dragon lake monster legend is associated with a lake in which country?
A) Finland
B) Norway
C) Russia
D) Sweden
Answer: C
Fact: Lake Brosno in the Tver region of Russia has legends of a monster dating back to the 13th century, reportedly appearing in Soviet-era expedition reports.

## H8
Q: The real-life 2013 rooftop water tank tragedy that fuelled urban legend took place at which Los Angeles hotel?
A) Chateau Marmont
B) Cecil Hotel
C) Beverly Hilton
D) Roosevelt Hotel
Answer: B
Fact: Elisa Lam's body was found in the Cecil Hotel's rooftop water tank in 2013, reviving interest in the long-troubled hotel's dark history.

## H9
Q: The myth that hair and nails keep growing after death is caused by which optical effect?
A) Swelling
B) Retraction
C) Discolouration
D) Hardening
Answer: B
Fact: After death, skin dehydrates and retracts, making hair and nails appear longer; this optical illusion gave rise to the persistent growth myth.

## H10
Q: In Slavic legend, which male water spirit lures humans to drown in rivers and lakes?
A) Leshy
B) Domovoi
C) Vodyanoy
D) Dvorovoi
Answer: C
Fact: The Vodyanoy is said to appear as a frog-like old man covered in algae and moss, sometimes shapeshifting into fish or humans to claim victims.
