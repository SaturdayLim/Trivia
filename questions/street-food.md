# Category: Street Food

## E1
Q: Which country is Pad Thai originally from?
A) Vietnam
B) Thailand
C) Malaysia
D) Cambodia
Answer: B
Fact: Pad Thai was popularised in the 1940s as part of a national campaign to reduce rice consumption.

## E2
Q: What is a Pretzel?
A) Fried dough
B) Rice cake
C) Baked bread
D) Steamed bun
Answer: C
Fact: The distinctive knot shape of a pretzel is said to represent arms folded in prayer.

## E3
Q: Tacos al Pastor originated in which country?
A) Spain
B) Mexico
C) Colombia
D) Argentina
Answer: B
Fact: The dish was inspired by Lebanese shawarma brought to Mexico by Lebanese immigrants in the early 20th century.

## E4
Q: What is the main protein in a traditional Hotdog?
A) Chicken
B) Lamb
C) Pork
D) Tuna
Answer: C
Fact: Hotdogs were popularised in the USA in the late 1800s by German immigrant butchers.

## E5
Q: Originally called 'Fairy Floss', what is this spun-sugar treat now known as?
A) Candy Floss
B) Cotton Candy
C) Toffee Apple
D) Spun Sugar
Answer: B
Fact: The name 'Fairy Floss' is still commonly used in Australia.

## E6
Q: Banh Mi is a street food sandwich from which country?
A) China
B) Japan
C) Vietnam
D) Korea
Answer: C
Fact: The baguette-style bread used in Banh Mi is a lasting influence of French colonial rule in Vietnam.

## E7
Q: Samosa is a popular street snack that is typically what type of dish?
A) Grilled skewer
B) Fried pastry
C) Steamed dumpling
D) Rice bowl
Answer: B
Fact: Samosas are believed to have originated in the Middle East before spreading to South Asia via trade routes.

## E8
Q: Churros are most associated with which country?
A) France
B) Italy
C) Portugal
D) Spain
Answer: D
Fact: Churros are commonly dipped in thick hot chocolate, especially as a breakfast snack in Spain.

## E9
Q: Satay skewers are closely associated with street food culture in which region?
A) South America
B) West Africa
C) Southeast Asia
D) Middle East
Answer: C
Fact: Satay is particularly popular in Indonesia, Malaysia, and Singapore, often served with peanut sauce.

## E10
Q: Traditional British Fish and Chips — what is the coating on the fish called?
A) Breading
B) Tempura
C) Batter
D) Crumb
Answer: C
Fact: Fish and chips shops became widespread in Britain during the 1800s as a cheap and filling meal for factory workers.

## M1
Q: What flour is traditionally used to make Japanese Takoyaki batter?
A) Rice flour
B) Wheat flour
C) Buckwheat flour
D) Cornflour
Answer: B
Fact: Takoyaki, meaning 'octopus balls', originated in Osaka in the 1930s and remains the city's most iconic street snack.

## M2
Q: Jerk chicken is a spicy grilled dish originating from which country?
A) Haiti
B) Trinidad
C) Jamaica
D) Barbados
Answer: C
Fact: The word 'jerk' likely derives from the Quechua word 'charqui', referring to dried, spiced meat.

## M3
Q: Halo-halo, a popular Filipino street dessert, has what as its base layer?
A) Coconut cream
B) Shaved ice
C) Mango sorbet
D) Rice porridge
Answer: B
Fact: Halo-halo means 'mix-mix' in Filipino, and recipes vary widely — toppings can include sweet beans, jelly, and ube ice cream.

## M4
Q: Arepas are a staple street food in which two South American countries?
A) Peru & Ecuador
B) Brazil & Bolivia
C) Colombia & Venezuela
D) Chile & Argentina
Answer: C
Fact: Arepas are made from ground maize dough and have been eaten in the region for thousands of years.

## M5
Q: Okonomiyaki, a popular Japanese street food, is best described as what?
A) Savoury pancake
B) Rice ball
C) Pork bun
D) Noodle soup
Answer: A
Fact: The name Okonomiyaki roughly translates to 'grilled as you like it', reflecting its highly customisable toppings.

## M6
Q: Bunny chow, a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry, comes from which city?
A) Cape Town
B) Johannesburg
C) Durban
D) Nairobi
Answer: C
Fact: Bunny chow was created by the Indian community in Durban as a portable meal for labourers who had no cutlery.

## M7

A) Turmeric alone
B) Curry powder
C) Dried shrimp paste
D) Coconut milk
Answer: D
Fact: Laksa is a spicy coconut-milk noodle soup considered one of Singapore's most beloved national dishes.

A) Guatemala

C) El Salvador
D) Nicaragua
Answer: C
Fact: Pupusas date back over 2,000 years to the indigenous Pipil people of El Salvador.

## M9
Q: Turkish Simit is a popular street snack shaped like a ring and coated in what seeds?
A) Poppy
B) Sesame
C) Sunflower

Answer: B
Fact: Simit has been sold by street vendors in Istanbul since the 16th century and is often eaten for breakfast.

## M10
Q: Nasi Lemak, often called Malaysia's national dish, gets its name from what?
A) Rich rice
B) Spicy leaf
C) Coconut leaf
D) Fragrant prawn
Answer: A
Fact: Nasi Lemak means 'rich rice' in Malay, referring to the rice being cooked in coconut cream.

## H1
Q: Which spice is the primary flavouring in Egyptian Kushari's tomato sauce?
A) Saffron
B) Cumin
C) Coriander seed
D) Fenugreek
Answer: B
Fact: Kushari is Egypt's unofficial national dish, combining lentils, rice, pasta, and a spiced tomato sauce in one bowl.

## H2
Q: Tteok-bokki's modern spicy style was born in which neighbourhood of Seoul?
A) Hongdae
B) Itaewon
C) Sindang-dong
D) Insadong
Answer: C
Fact: The gochujang-sauced Tteok-bokki we know today became popular in Sindang-dong, Seoul, in the 1950s.

## H3
Q: Cevapi, small grilled minced-meat sausages, are the signature street food of which country?
A) Bulgaria
B) Croatia
C) Bosnia
D) Albania
Answer: C
Fact: Cevapi are considered Bosnia's national dish and are typically served in a flatbread called lepinja with raw onion and sour cream.

## H4
Q: Banh Cuon, a Vietnamese steamed roll, is made with batter from what type of flour?
A) Tapioca
B) Rice
C) Mung bean
D) Cassava
Answer: B
Fact: Banh Cuon sheets are steamed in seconds on a thin cloth stretched over boiling water, making them paper-thin.

## H5
Q: Shawarma takes its name from the Turkish word 'cevirme', which means what?
A) Roasting
B) Marinating
C) Turning
D) Slicing
Answer: C
Fact: The rotating vertical spit that cooks shawarma meat gives the dish its name — 'turning' the meat slowly over heat.

## H6
Q: What is the key souring agent in Mumbai's Pani Puri water?
A) Dried mango
B) Tamarind
C) Kokum
D) Green mango
Answer: B
Fact: Pani Puri water recipes vary by region across India — Mumbai versions use tamarind while Delhi prefers a mint-heavy blend.

## H7
Q: Khachapuri, a cheese-filled bread boat topped with an egg, is the signature street food of which country?
A) Armenia
B) Azerbaijan
C) Georgia
D) Ukraine
Answer: C
Fact: The Adjarian style of Khachapuri, shaped like an open boat with a runny egg on top, comes from the coastal region of Adjara.

## H8
Q: What cooking technique defines Peruvian Anticuchos skewers?
A) Slow braised
B) Charcoal grilled
C) Deep fried
D) Steamed
Answer: B
Fact: Anticuchos are traditionally made from beef heart, marinated in vinegar and aji panca chilli before grilling.

## H9
Q: Which ingredient gives Thai Boat Noodle broth its distinctive deep, dark colour?
A) Dark soy
B) Shrimp paste
C) Pig blood
D) Tamarind
Answer: C
Fact: Thai Boat Noodles originated from vendors selling noodles from small boats along Bangkok's canals.

## H10
Q: Currywurst, sliced sausage with curry-spiced ketchup, was invented in which German city?
A) Munich
B) Hamburg
C) Berlin
D) Frankfurt
Answer: C
Fact: Currywurst was invented by Herta Heuwer in Berlin in 1949, reportedly after she obtained ketchup and curry powder from British soldiers.
