# Category: Islands

## E1
Q: Which of these is where the Eiffel Tower actually stands?
A) Corsica
B) Madagascar
C) Mainland
D) Réunion
Answer: C
Fact: Paris sits on the French mainland; the Île de la Cité inside the Seine is too small to be counted among France's famous islands.

## E2
Q: Which island nation is known as the 'Land of Fire and Ice'?
A) Greenland
B) Iceland
C) Norway
D) Faroe Islands
Answer: B
Fact: Iceland has over 130 volcanoes and around 11% of its surface is covered by glaciers, earning it the nickname.

## E3
Q: Copacabana Beach is located in which city?
A) Buenos Aires
B) Lima
C) Rio
D) Montevideo
Answer: C
Fact: Copacabana Beach stretches over 4 kilometres along Rio's southern coast and is one of the most visited beaches in the world.

## E4
Q: Which is the largest island in the world?
A) Australia
B) Greenland
C) Borneo
D) New Guinea
Answer: B
Fact: Greenland covers about 2.16 million square kilometres, though Australia is larger, it is classified as a continent rather than an island.

## E5
Q: The island of Cuba is located in which body of water?
A) Pacific Ocean
B) Mediterranean
C) Caribbean Sea
D) Atlantic
Answer: C
Fact: Cuba is the largest island in the Caribbean and is situated at the entrance to the Gulf of Mexico.

## E6
Q: Bali is a popular holiday island belonging to which country?
A) Thailand
B) Malaysia
C) Philippines
D) Indonesia
Answer: D
Fact: Bali is one of over 17,000 islands that make up Indonesia, the world's largest archipelago nation.

## E7
Q: Which island is called the 'Emerald Isle'?
A) Iceland
B) Ireland
C) Sri Lanka
D) Jamaica
Answer: B
Fact: Ireland earned its nickname due to its lush green countryside, kept verdant by the island's frequent rainfall.

## E8
Q: Bondi Beach is a world-famous beach located in which city?
A) Auckland
B) Cape Town
C) Sydney
D) Melbourne
Answer: C
Fact: Bondi Beach is only about 8 kilometres from Sydney's CBD and attracts around 2.5 million visitors per year.

## E9
Q: Hawaii is a state that belongs to which country?
A) Canada
B) Australia
C) USA
D) Mexico
Answer: C
Fact: Hawaii became the 50th and most recent US state to join the union in 1959, and is the only US state made entirely of islands.

## E10
Q: Which island is famous for its giant stone statues called Moai?
A) Fiji
B) Tahiti
C) Easter Island
D) Samoa
Answer: C
Fact: Easter Island, known locally as Rapa Nui, has nearly 1,000 Moai statues carved by the indigenous Rapa Nui people.

## M1
Q: The Maldives archipelago sits in which ocean?
A) Pacific Ocean
B) Atlantic Ocean
C) Indian Ocean
D) Southern Ocean
Answer: C
Fact: The Maldives is the world's lowest-lying country, with an average ground elevation of just 1.5 metres above sea level.

## M2
Q: Which island is nicknamed the 'Pearl of the Orient Seas'?
A) Taiwan
B) Sri Lanka
C) Philippines
D) Singapore
Answer: C
Fact: The nickname was coined by Spanish explorers who admired the natural beauty and resources of the Philippine archipelago.

## M3
Q: The Galápagos Islands are a territory of which country?
A) Peru
B) Colombia
C) Ecuador
D) Chile
Answer: C
Fact: Charles Darwin's observations of Galápagos wildlife during his 1835 visit helped inspire his theory of evolution by natural selection.

## M4
Q: The famous pink-sand Horseshoe Bay Beach is located in which island territory?
A) Bahamas
B) Bermuda
C) Barbados
D) Cayman Islands
Answer: B
Fact: Bermuda's pink sand gets its colour from fragments of red foraminifera shells mixed with white coral and sand.

## M5
Q: Sardinia is an autonomous island region belonging to which country?
A) France
B) Greece
C) Spain
D) Italy
Answer: D
Fact: Sardinia is the second-largest island in the Mediterranean Sea and is home to the ancient Nuragic civilisation's stone towers.

## M6
Q: Sri Lanka is sometimes called the 'Teardrop of' which larger landmass?
A) Asia
B) Arabia
C) India
D) Africa
Answer: C
Fact: Sri Lanka's teardrop shape and location just off the southern tip of India inspired the poetic nickname used since ancient times.

## M7
Q: The Canary Islands belong to which country despite being near the African coast?
A) Portugal
B) Morocco
C) Spain
D) France
Answer: C
Fact: The Canary Islands are closer to Africa than to mainland Spain, yet have been a Spanish territory since the 15th century.

## M8
Q: Corsica, the birthplace of Napoleon Bonaparte, belongs to which country?
A) Italy
B) France
C) Monaco
D) Spain
Answer: B
Fact: Napoleon was born in Ajaccio, Corsica, in 1769, just a year after France purchased the island from the Republic of Genoa.

## M9
Q: Whitehaven Beach, consistently ranked among the world's best beaches, is in which country?
A) New Zealand
B) Philippines
C) Australia
D) Fiji
Answer: C
Fact: Whitehaven Beach on Whitsunday Island is famous for its 98% pure silica sand, which stays cool even in direct sunlight.

## M10
Q: The island of Borneo is shared between how many countries?
A) Two
B) Three
C) Four
D) Five
Answer: B
Fact: Borneo is divided among Malaysia, Indonesia, and Brunei, making it one of the few islands in the world shared by three nations.

## H1
Q: Which Caribbean island is nicknamed 'La Isla del Encanto' (Island of Enchantment)?
A) Cuba
B) Puerto Rico
C) Hispaniola
D) Jamaica
Answer: B
Fact: The nickname was popularised by Puerto Rican poet Manuel Fernández Juncos in the late 19th century and remains the island's official motto.

## H2
Q: Which archipelago in the South Atlantic is also known as the Malvinas?
A) Azores
B) Falkland Islands
C) South Georgia
D) Ascension
Answer: B
Fact: Argentina calls the islands the Malvinas and went to war with Britain over their sovereignty in 1982, a conflict lasting 74 days.

## H3
Q: The island of Socotra, famous for its dragon blood trees, belongs to which country?
A) Oman
B) Somalia
C) Yemen
D) Ethiopia
Answer: C
Fact: Socotra is sometimes called the 'Galápagos of the Indian Ocean' because over a third of its plant species exist nowhere else on Earth.

## H4
Q: Nungwi Beach, known for its white sand and dhow boat-building, is on which island?
A) Mauritius
B) Zanzibar
C) Seychelles
D) Comoros
Answer: B
Fact: Nungwi sits at Zanzibar's northern tip and has been a centre of traditional dhow craftsmanship for centuries due to its tidal pools.

## H5
Q: Which island in the Indian Ocean is nicknamed the 'Star and Key of the Indian Ocean'?
A) Réunion
B) Madagascar
C) Mauritius
D) Sri Lanka
Answer: C
Fact: The nickname appears on Mauritius's coat of arms and dates to the island's strategic importance as a naval base in the 18th century.

## H6
Q: The Lakshadweep Islands are a union territory of which country?
A) Sri Lanka
B) Maldives
C) India
D) Bangladesh
Answer: C
Fact: Lakshadweep consists of 36 islands in the Arabian Sea, but only 10 of them are permanently inhabited by people.

## H7
Q: Ilha de Marajó, one of the world's largest river islands, sits in the mouth of which river?
A) Orinoco
B) Amazon
C) Paraná
D) Congo
Answer: B
Fact: Marajó Island in Brazil is roughly the size of Switzerland and is home to large herds of water buffalo introduced in colonial times.

## H8
Q: The Åland Islands, an autonomous Swedish-speaking territory, belong to which country?
A) Sweden
B) Norway
C) Finland
D) Denmark
Answer: C
Fact: The Åland Islands are demilitarised under international treaty and have their own parliament, stamps, and vehicle registration plates.

## H9
Q: Radhanagar Beach, awarded Asia's Best Beach in 2004 by Time magazine, is on which Indian island?
A) Lakshadweep
B) Andaman
C) Nicobar
D) Daman
Answer: B
Fact: Radhanagar Beach on Havelock Island in the Andamans is famed for its turquoise water and the dense tropical forest running to its shore.

## H10
Q: Which Pacific island group is nicknamed the 'Crossroads of Polynesia'?
A) Tonga
B) Samoa
C) Tuvalu
D) Cook Islands
Answer: B
Fact: Samoa's central location in the South Pacific made it a key navigation and trading hub for Polynesian voyagers for thousands of years.
