## E1
Q: How many days are there in a leap year?
A) 364
B) 365
C) 366
D) 367
Answer: C
Fact: Leap years keep our calendar lined up with Earth's orbit around the Sun.

## E2
Q: What is the capital city of France?
A) Paris
B) Rome
C) Madrid
D) Berlin
Answer: A
Fact: Paris has been France's capital for over 1,000 years.

## E3
Q: How many continents are there on Earth?
A) 5
B) 6
C) 7
D) 8
Answer: C
Fact: Some geographers group Europe and Asia together as one, giving six instead.

## E4
Q: What color do you get by mixing blue and yellow paint?
A) Purple
B) Green
C) Orange
D) Brown
Answer: B
Fact: Green sits between blue and yellow on the color wheel.

## M1
Q: Which planet is known as the Red Planet?
A) Venus
B) Mars
C) Jupiter
D) Saturn
Answer: B
Fact: Iron oxide, or rust, on its surface gives Mars its reddish color.

## M2
Q: What is the largest ocean on Earth?
A) Atlantic Ocean
B) Indian Ocean
C) Arctic Ocean
D) Pacific Ocean
Answer: D
Fact: The Pacific covers more area than all of Earth's continents combined.

## M3
Q: In which year did the Titanic sink?
A) 1905
B) 1912
C) 1920
D) 1931
Answer: B
Fact: The wreck itself was not found until 1985, over 70 years later.

## M4
Q: What is the smallest prime number?
A) 0
B) 1
C) 2
D) 3
Answer: C
Fact: Two is also the only even prime number.

## H1
Q: What is the chemical symbol for gold?
A) Go
B) Gd
C) Au
D) Ag
Answer: C
Fact: "Au" comes from the Latin word for gold, "aurum."

## H2
Q: Which country has the most natural lakes?
A) United States
B) Russia
C) Finland
D) Canada

Fact: Canada is home to roughly 60% of the world's lakes.
