# Category: One-Hits

## E1
Q: "MMMBop" was the only big hit for which group?
A) Hanson
B) Boyz II Men
C) 98 Degrees
D) LFO
Answer: A
Fact: Hanson were brothers aged 11, 14, and 16 when "MMMBop" hit number one in 1997.

## E2
Q: Who sang "Take On Me" in 1985?
A) Erasure
B) a-ha
C) Pet Shop Boys
D) Alphaville
Answer: B
Fact: The iconic pencil-sketch music video for "Take On Me" took 16 weeks to produce and won six MTV Video Music Awards.

## E3
Q: "Macarena" was a global hit for which act?
A) Ace of Base
B) La Bouche
C) Los Del Rio
D) Corona
Answer: C
Fact: "Macarena" spent 14 weeks at number one on the US Billboard Hot 100, one of the longest runs in chart history.

## E4
Q: "Come On Eileen" was a massive hit for which band?
A) Madness
B) Dexys Midnight Runners
C) The Specials
D) The Beat
Answer: B
Fact: "Come On Eileen" was the best-selling single of 1982 in both the UK and the United States.

## E5
Q: Which artist had a one-hit wonder with "Mambo No. 5"?
A) Ricky Martin
B) Marc Anthony
C) Lou Bega
D) Enrique Iglesias
Answer: C
Fact: Lou Bega based "Mambo No. 5" on a 1949 instrumental by Cuban musician Damaso Perez Prado.

## E6
Q: "Who Let the Dogs Out" was the signature hit of which group?
A) Shaggy
B) Baha Men
C) Ini Kamoze
D) Sean Paul
Answer: B
Fact: "Who Let the Dogs Out" won the Grammy Award for Best Dance Recording in 2001.

## E7
Q: "Spirit in the Sky" is the one hit of which artist?
A) Norman Greenbaum
B) Randy Newman
C) Harry Nilsson
D) Todd Rundgren
Answer: A
Fact: Norman Greenbaum wrote "Spirit in the Sky" in about 15 minutes and it became a top-five hit in 1970.

## E8
Q: "Cotton Eye Joe" was a hit for which act?
A) Rednex
B) Haddaway
C) 2 Unlimited
D) Snap!
Answer: A
Fact: Rednex was a Swedish group who blended American country folk music with Eurodance to create their signature sound.

## E9
Q: "99 Luftballons" was sung by which German artist?
A) Falco
B) Nena
C) Sandra
D) Modern Talking
Answer: B
Fact: "99 Luftballons" is one of the rare songs to reach the top ten in both its original German version and an English translation.

## E10
Q: "I'm Too Sexy" was a hit for which act?
A) Right Said Fred
B) Bros
C) Curiosity Killed the Cat
D) Big Fun
Answer: A
Fact: Right Said Fred are brothers Richard and Fred Fairbrass, who named the band after a 1962 comedy song by Bernard Cribbins.

## M1
Q: "Tainted Love" is forever linked to which act?
A) Soft Cell
B) Human League
C) Depeche Mode
D) OMD
Answer: A
Fact: "Tainted Love" was originally recorded by Gloria Jones in 1964, and Soft Cell's synth-pop cover spent 43 weeks on the UK charts.

## M2
Q: "Seasons in the Sun" gave one major hit to which singer?
A) Gilbert O'Sullivan
B) Terry Jacks
C) David Cassidy
D) Donny Osmond
Answer: B
Fact: "Seasons in the Sun" is an English adaptation of the Belgian song "Le Moribond" written by Jacques Brel in 1961.

## M3
Q: "Flagpole Sitta" is the cult one-hit of which band?
A) Semisonic
B) Harvey Danger
C) Fastball
D) Primitive Radio Gods
Answer: B
Fact: Harvey Danger distributed "Flagpole Sitta" for free online in 1997, making it an early example of viral music promotion.

## M4
Q: "Sunglasses at Night" was the signature song of which artist?
A) Corey Hart
B) John Parr
C) Paul Engemann
D) Pseudo Echo
Answer: A
Fact: Corey Hart is a Canadian singer, and "Sunglasses at Night" reached number seven on the US Billboard Hot 100 in 1984.

## M5
Q: "Judy in Disguise" was a 1967 hit for which act?
A) Tommy James
B) John Fred
C) Wayne Fontana
D) Gary Lewis
Answer: B
Fact: "Judy in Disguise (With Glasses)" by John Fred and His Playboy Band was a parody of the Beatles' "Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds".

## M6
Q: "Achy Breaky Heart" launched which singer briefly into pop stardom?
A) Travis Tritt
B) Billy Ray Cyrus
C) Garth Brooks
D) Clint Black
Answer: B
Fact: "Achy Breaky Heart" was the first single ever to go platinum in Australia and helped spark the line-dancing craze of the early 1990s.

## M7
Q: The English-language cover of 'Der Commissar' was the sole hit for which British act?
A) Opus
B) Falco
C) After the Fire
D) Alphaville
Answer: C
Fact: After the Fire's English cover of 'Der Commissar' reached the US Top 5 in 1983, but the band never charted again.

## M8
Q: "What Is Love" is the defining hit of which act?
A) Corona
B) Haddaway
C) Real McCoy
D) Culture Beat
Answer: B
Fact: Haddaway is a Trinidad-born singer based in Germany, and "What Is Love" became one of the best-selling singles of 1993 worldwide.

## M9
Q: "Fly Robin Fly" was the sole smash hit for which group?
A) Silver Convention
B) MFSB
C) Heatwave
D) Boney M
Answer: A
Fact: "Fly Robin Fly" contains only six words across its entire lyrics and still won the Grammy for Best Rhythm and Blues Song in 1976.

## M10
Q: 'Steal My Sunshine' was the one major hit for which Canadian act?
A) Mungo Jerry
B) Len
C) Tal Bachman
D) Prozzak
Answer: B
Fact: Len's 'Steal My Sunshine' sampled Andrea True Connection's 1976 disco track 'More More More' and hit the top 10 in 1999.

## H1
Q: "Winchester Cathedral" was the novelty hit for which act?
A) Hermans Hermits
B) New Vaudeville Band
C) Freddie and Dreamers
D) Dave Clark Five
Answer: B
Fact: "Winchester Cathedral" used a megaphone effect to recreate the sound of 1920s crooners and reached number one in the US in 1966.

## H2
Q: "Turning Japanese" was the one chart hit for which British band?
A) The Vapors
B) The Undertones
C) The Rezillos
D) The Boomtown Rats
Answer: A
Fact: The Vapors were a British new-wave band from Guildford, and "Turning Japanese" reached number three in the UK in 1980.

## H3
Q: "Breakfast at Tiffany's" is the lone hit of which band?
A) Primitive Radio Gods
B) Toadies
C) Deep Blue Something
D) Sponge
Answer: C
Fact: Deep Blue Something are from Denton, Texas, and their singer wrote "Breakfast at Tiffany's" after watching the Audrey Hepburn film.

## H4
Q: "Convoy" was the CB-radio novelty hit for which artist?
A) Red Sovine
B) C.W. McCall
C) Bobby Bare
D) Roger Miller
Answer: B
Fact: "Convoy" inspired the 1978 Sam Peckinpah film of the same name and reached number one in both the US and UK in 1976.

## H5
Q: "Major Tom (Coming Home)" was the sole global hit for which German singer?
A) Nena
B) Klaus Nomi
C) Peter Schilling
D) Extrabreit
Answer: C
Fact: Peter Schilling's "Major Tom (Coming Home)" is a sequel to David Bowie's "Space Oddity", told from Major Tom's perspective adrift in space.

## H6
Q: "Stay" was the one charting smash for which early 60s act?
A) Maurice Williams
B) Ben E. King
C) Curtis Lee
D) Gene Chandler
Answer: A
Fact: At one minute and 37 seconds, Maurice Williams' "Stay" is considered one of the shortest songs ever to reach number one in the US.

## H7
Q: "Informer" was the one breakthrough hit for which Canadian rapper?
A) Chilly Tee
B) Snow
C) Maestro Fresh Wes
D) Michie Mee
Answer: B
Fact: Snow recorded "Informer" while on bail for assault charges, and the song's rapid patois delivery baffled listeners who tried to decode the lyrics.

## H8
Q: "Hocus Pocus" was the sole hit for which Dutch prog-rock band?
A) Shocking Blue
B) Golden Earring
C) Focus
D) Earth and Fire
Answer: C
Fact: "Hocus Pocus" is notable for its yodelling and scatting by vocalist Thijs van Leer, making it one of rock's most bizarre chart hits.

## H9
Q: "She Drives Me Crazy" was the standout hit for which British act?
A) Talk Talk
B) Fine Young Cannibals
C) Furniture
D) Book of Love
Answer: B
Fact: Fine Young Cannibals were formed by two former members of The English Beat, and Roland Gift's falsetto became their instantly recognisable signature.

## H10
Q: "We Like to Party" was the anthem of which Dutch act?
A) Vengaboys
B) 2 Brothers on the 4th Floor
C) Alice Deejay
D) Rollergirl
Answer: A
Fact: The Vengaboys were assembled by two Dutch DJs and their colourful tour bus became a pop-culture icon of the late 1990s Eurodance era.
