# Category: Desserts

## E1
Q: Cotton candy was originally called what?
A) Fairy Floss
B) Sugar Cloud
C) Spun Sweet
D) Angel Floss
Answer: A
Fact: Cotton candy was invented in 1897 and debuted at the 1904 World's Fair under the name Fairy Floss.

## E2
Q: Which country invented gelato?
A) France
B) Italy
C) Spain
D) Greece
Answer: B
Fact: Gelato has less fat than regular ice cream because it uses more milk than cream and fewer egg yolks.

## E3
Q: What is the main flavour in a Snickers bar?
A) Coconut
B) Caramel
C) Peanut
D) Almond
Answer: C
Fact: Snickers was named after the Mars family's favourite horse and has been the world's best-selling candy bar for decades.

## E4
Q: Which country is the croissant most associated with?
A) Belgium
B) Germany
C) France
D) Austria
Answer: C
Fact: The croissant's crescent shape was actually invented in Vienna, Austria, but France made it famous with buttery, flaky pastry.

## E5
Q: What shape is a traditional Korean Choco Pie?
A) Square
B) Triangle
C) Round
D) Star
Answer: C
Fact: Choco Pies are iconic Korean snack cakes made of marshmallow sandwiched between two round chocolate-coated biscuits.

## E6
Q: What colour is a classic creme brulee topping after finishing?
A) White
B) Golden Brown
C) Black
D) Pink
Answer: B
Fact: The caramelised sugar crust on creme brulee is made by torching or grilling granulated sugar until it melts and crisps.

## E7
Q: What fruit is layered in a classic Black Forest cake?
A) Strawberry
B) Cherry
C) Raspberry
D) Peach
Answer: B
Fact: The German Black Forest cake traditionally contains Kirsch, a cherry brandy from the Black Forest region.

## E8
Q: Mochi is a Japanese sweet made from what?
A) Wheat Flour
B) Rice
C) Tapioca
D) Potato
Answer: B
Fact: Mochi is made by pounding glutinous rice into a sticky, stretchy dough, and is eaten during Japanese New Year celebrations.

## E9
Q: What is the classic primary flavour of Turkish Delight?
A) Lemon
B) Rose
C) Mint
D) Vanilla
Answer: B
Fact: Turkish Delight, or lokum, has been made in Turkey since the 15th century and is traditionally flavoured with rosewater.

## E10
Q: Which flavour is the third stripe in a Neapolitan ice cream block?
A) Mint
B) Strawberry
C) Lemon
D) Coffee
Answer: B
Fact: Neapolitan ice cream always features three stripes — chocolate, vanilla, and strawberry — side by side in one block.

## M1
Q: Traditional baklava is most commonly filled with chopped what?
A) Almonds
B) Pistachios
C) Walnuts
D) Cashews
Answer: C
Fact: While pistachio versions exist, the most traditional baklava uses walnuts layered between filo pastry and drenched in honey syrup.

## M2
Q: What is the key base ingredient in Filipino halo-halo?
A) Coconut Milk
B) Shaved Ice
C) Tapioca
D) Jackfruit
Answer: B
Fact: Halo-halo means mix-mix in Filipino, and the shaved ice base is piled with colourful toppings like beans, jelly, and ube.

## M3
Q: Which country is tiramisu originally from?
A) France
B) Italy
C) Portugal
D) Spain
Answer: B
Fact: Tiramisu was created in the Veneto region of Italy in the 1960s and its name means lift me up in Italian.

## M4
Q: Kue lapis is a layered steamed cake from which country?
A) Thailand
B) Vietnam
C) Indonesia
D) Malaysia
Answer: C
Fact: Kue lapis is made with rice flour and coconut milk, and its alternating coloured layers are steamed one at a time.

## M5
Q: What is the Japanese ingredient that gives matcha ice cream its vivid green colour and earthy flavour?
A) Pandan
B) Spirulina
C) Matcha
D) Pistachio
Answer: C
Fact: Matcha is finely ground powder of specially grown shade-dried green tea leaves and gives ice cream a vivid colour and a gently bitter, earthy taste.

## M6
Q: Gulab jamun is soaked in which liquid after frying?
A) Honey
B) Rose Syrup
C) Sugar Syrup
D) Condensed Milk
Answer: C
Fact: Gulab jamun is a fried milk-solid dumpling soaked in a sugar syrup flavoured with cardamom, rosewater, and saffron.

## M7
Q: Panna cotta is traditionally made with which setting agent?
A) Agar
B) Cornstarch
C) Gelatin
D) Pectin
Answer: C
Fact: Panna cotta means cooked cream in Italian and sets into a silky dessert with just enough gelatin to hold its shape when unmoulded.

## M8
Q: A Paris-Brest is a classic French pastry made from the same dough as profiteroles — which dough is that?
A) Shortcrust
B) Filo
C) Choux
D) Puff
Answer: C
Fact: The Paris-Brest was created in 1910 to celebrate a famous Paris–Brest–Paris bicycle race; its ring shape mimics a bicycle wheel.

## M9
Q: Tang yuan glutinous rice balls are traditionally eaten during which Chinese festival?
A) Dragon Boat
B) Lantern Festival
C) Mid-Autumn
D) Qingming
Answer: B
Fact: Tang yuan's round shape symbolises family unity and they are the traditional food of the Lantern Festival, marking the end of Chinese New Year.

## M10
Q: What nut is traditional marzipan made from?
A) Hazelnut
B) Cashew
C) Almond
D) Pistachio
Answer: C
Fact: Marzipan is a paste of finely ground almonds and sugar, originally from the Middle East and brought to Europe during the medieval period.

## H1
Q: Which French dessert is made by caramelising butter and sugar at the tin bottom before baking?
A) Kouign-amann
B) Tarte Tatin
C) Clafoutis
D) Financier
Answer: B
Fact: Tarte Tatin was accidentally created in the 1880s when Stephanie Tatin left apples cooking in butter and sugar too long and covered them with pastry.

## H2
Q: Ondeh-ondeh are pandan-flavoured balls filled with what?
A) Coconut Jam
B) Red Bean
C) Palm Sugar
D) Durian
Answer: C
Fact: When you bite into ondeh-ondeh, the melted palm sugar bursts out in a sweet liquid rush before you taste the grated coconut coating.

## H3
Q: What is the key filling in Taiwanese sun cakes, taiyang bing?
A) Red Bean
B) Maltose
C) Sesame
D) Taro
Answer: B
Fact: Taiyang bing from Taichung have a flaky pastry shell filled with a chewy, lightly sweet maltose centre and are a famous Taiwanese souvenir snack.

## H4
Q: Chhena poda, India's only traditional baked dessert, originates from which state?
A) Bengal
B) Gujarat
C) Odisha
D) Kerala
Answer: C
Fact: Chhena poda means burnt cheese in Odia and is made from cottage cheese caramelised by baking, creating a darkened, flavourful crust.

## H5
Q: Kouign-amann caramelised pastry originates from which region of France?
A) Normandy
B) Brittany
C) Alsace
D) Bordeaux
Answer: B
Fact: Kouign-amann means butter cake in Breton and was invented in 1860 in Douarnenez by a baker who folded butter and sugar into bread dough.

## H6
Q: What is the primary cheese used in traditional Levantine kunafa?
A) Halloumi
B) Ricotta
C) Akkawi
D) Labneh
Answer: C
Fact: Akkawi cheese is desalted before use in kunafa so it melts smoothly under the shredded kataifi dough without being overpoweringly salty.

## H7
Q: What type of bean is used to make traditional Japanese yokan jelly?
A) Black Bean
B) Adzuki Bean
C) Mung Bean
D) Edamame
Answer: B
Fact: Yokan is a firm jelly made from adzuki bean paste, agar, and sugar, and has been a popular Japanese wagashi since the Edo period.

## H8
Q: Besides evaporated milk, which of these is also in tres leches cake's soaking mixture?
A) Oat Milk
B) Buttermilk
C) Condensed Milk
D) Goat Milk
Answer: C
Fact: The three milks are evaporated milk, sweetened condensed milk, and heavy cream, creating an ultra-moist sponge cake popular across Latin America.

## H9
Q: What Austrian pastry is made by stretching dough paper-thin and rolling it around a filling?
A) Strudel
B) Sachertorte
C) Linzer Torte
D) Palatschinken
Answer: A
Fact: Authentic strudel dough is stretched by hand until thin enough to read a newspaper through before being rolled around the filling.

## H10
Q: Klepon, a green glutinous rice ball coated in coconut, originates from which country?
A) Philippines
B) Thailand
C) Indonesia
D) Cambodia
Answer: C
Fact: Klepon gets its green colour from pandan juice and bursts with liquid palm sugar when bitten, making it a beloved Indonesian street snack.
