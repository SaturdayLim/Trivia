## E1
Q: How many legs does a spider have?
A) 6
B) 8
C) 10
D) 12
Answer: B
Fact: Spiders are arachnids, not insects, which is why they have eight legs.

## E2
Q: What gas do plants absorb from the air to make their own food?
A) Oxygen
B) Nitrogen
C) Carbon dioxide
D) Hydrogen
Answer: C
Fact: Plants combine carbon dioxide with sunlight and water through photosynthesis.

## E3
Q: What is the largest land animal alive today?
A) Giraffe
B) African elephant
C) Rhinoceros
D) Hippopotamus
Answer: B
Fact: A male African elephant can weigh more than 6,000 kilograms.

## E4
Q: Which part of a plant absorbs water from the soil?
A) Leaves
B) Petals
C) Roots
D) Stem
Answer: C
Fact: Tiny root hairs greatly increase the surface area for soaking up water.

## M1
Q: What is the closest star to Earth?
A) Proxima Centauri
B) The Sun
C) Sirius
D) Polaris
Answer: B
Fact: Light from the Sun takes about eight minutes to reach Earth.

## M2
Q: How many chambers does a human heart have?
A) 2
B) 3
C) 4
D) 5
Answer: C
Fact: Two chambers are called atria and two are called ventricles.

## M3
Q: What is the hardest naturally occurring substance on Earth?
A) Quartz
B) Diamond
C) Granite
D) Steel
Answer: B
Fact: Diamonds form under intense heat and pressure deep underground.

## M4
Q: Which gas makes up most of Earth's atmosphere?
A) Oxygen
B) Carbon dioxide
C) Nitrogen
D) Hydrogen
Answer: C
Fact: Nitrogen makes up about 78% of the air we breathe.

## H1
Q: What is the process by which a caterpillar becomes a butterfly called?
A) Hibernation
B) Metamorphosis
C) Pollination
D) Germination
Answer: B
Fact: Inside the chrysalis, the caterpillar's body is almost entirely rebuilt.

## H2
Q: Which blood type is known as the universal donor?
A) Type A
B) Type B
C) Type AB
D) O negative
Answer: D
Fact: O negative red cells lack the markers that trigger rejection in most recipients.

## H3
Q: Which part of a cell contains its genetic material?
A) Mitochondria
B) Nucleus
C) Cytoplasm
D) Ribosome
Answer: B
Fact: DNA is coiled tightly into chromosomes inside the nucleus.

## H4
Q: What is the scientific study of fungi called?
A) Botany
B) Mycology
C) Zoology
D) Entomology
Answer: B
Fact: Mushrooms are just the fruiting bodies of much larger underground fungal networks.
